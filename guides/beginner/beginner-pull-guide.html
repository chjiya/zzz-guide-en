🎰 Understanding the ZZZ Gacha System

Before deciding which banners to pull, you need to understand how ZZZ's gacha system works. The system has three key mechanics that every new player should know:

Hard Pity: You are guaranteed an S-rank agent or W-Engine within 90 pulls on character banners and 80 pulls on W-Engine banners. Soft pity starts at pull 74 (character) and pull 65 (W-Engine), where the rate increases dramatically. Most S-ranks arrive between pulls 74-82.

50/50 System: On limited character banners, your first S-rank has a 50% chance to be the featured character. If you lose the 50/50 (get a standard banner S-rank instead), your next S-rank is guaranteed to be the featured character. This guarantee carries over between banners, so if you lose the 50/50 on one banner, you're guaranteed the featured character on your next limited banner pull.

Pity Carryover: Your pity count carries over between banners of the same type. If you did 40 pulls on a limited character banner without getting an S-rank, you'll have 40 pity on the next limited character banner. This does NOT carry over between character and W-Engine banners.

📋 Banner Types Explained

Exclusive Channel (Limited Character Banner): This is where you should spend the majority of your Polychromes. The featured S-rank character rotates every 3 weeks. At pity 90 (hard pity), you are guaranteed an S-rank. First S-rank is 50/50; if you lose it, the next is guaranteed.

W-Engine Channel (Limited Weapon Banner): Similar to the character banner but costs 80 pulls for hard pity and has a 75/25 split (75% chance to get the featured W-Engine). Important: Only pull on this banner if you already have the featured character and have a second team ready. W-Engines are less valuable than new characters.

Stable Channel (Standard Banner): Uses Master Tapes (blue) which can be earned for free. Do NOT spend Polychromes on this banner — ever. The standard banner gives random S-rank characters and W-Engines from a fixed pool. You get a free S-rank selector at pull 300 (do not waste it).

Beginner Banner: Costs 50% fewer pulls for the first 50 summons. Offers one random S-rank from the standard pool at the end. This is a good deal — use your free Master Tapes here first before the standard banner.

📊 Pull Priority List (Detailed)

Phase 1: First Month (New Player)

  1. Pull the Beginner Banner (costs half price for 50 pulls) — use only free Master Tapes, never Polychromes.
  2. Save all Polychromes for the Limited Character Banner. Do not spend on standard banner or W-Engine banner.
  3. Target Caesar as your first limited S-rank. She is the best character for new accounts because she makes every team better and provides massive survivability that new players need.
  4. Build your free characters: Piper, Lucy, Anby, Nicole. These four A-ranks can clear all early to mid-game content.

Phase 2: Second Month

  1. Target a second limited S-rank. If you got Caesar, go for Burnice or Yanagi next. If you got a different limited character first, prioritize Caesar on her rerun.
  2. Consider pulling one W-Engine for your main DPS — but only after you have two strong teams. A new character is always more valuable than a W-Engine.

Phase 3: Established Account (3+ Months)

  1. Mindscapes: Consider M1 for your key supports (Caesar M1 extends shield duration by 3 seconds). Burnice M1 and Yanagi M2 are the most valuable early mindscapes.
  2. Signature W-Engines: Pull for signatures on your main DPS characters. Each signature is roughly a 15-20% damage increase over the best A-rank option.
  3. Expand your roster: Pull new characters to enable different team archetypes (Disorder, burst, mono-element).

❓ FAQ

Q1: Should I pull on the W-Engine banner as a new player?

No. As a new player, you should not pull on the W-Engine banner at all. Characters are significantly more valuable than weapons because they unlock new team compositions and playstyles. The best A-rank W-Engines perform at 75-85% of their signature S-rank counterparts, which is more than sufficient for clearing all content. Only consider W-Engine pulls after you have at least 6-8 S-rank characters and two complete endgame teams.

Q2: What if Caesar isn't on the current banner?

If Caesar isn't currently featured, save your pulls for her rerun. She is the single most valuable character in ZZZ and is worth waiting for. In the meantime, build your account with the free characters (Piper, Lucy, Anby, Nicole, Billy) and save all your Polychromes. If you absolutely must pull something, Yanagi is the next best long-term investment, followed by Burnice.

Q3: How do I get more Master Tapes (blue pulls)?

Master Tapes are the currency for the standard banner and cannot be purchased with Polychromes. You earn them through: Battle Pass (5 per level, 50 per pass), character ascension rewards (each character gives 2-3 Master Tapes when ascended to certain levels), Signal Shop (monthly exchange of 5 Master Tapes for residual signals), and events (varying amounts). Do not convert Polychromes into Master Tapes — this is a trap.

Q4: Is the Battle Pass worth buying?

The $10 Battle Pass is worth buying if you plan to play ZZZ regularly. It provides approximately 4 extra pulls worth of Polychromes, 50 Master Tapes, 5 S-rank W-Engine boxes, and significant upgrade materials. The $20 premium pass adds a title and name card but is not worth the extra cost. Compared to other gacha games, ZZZ's Battle Pass is above average in value but still optional — a F2P player can clear all content without it.
