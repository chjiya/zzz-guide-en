🤔 Is Rerolling Worth It in 2025?

Short answer: It depends on your patience. Rerolling in ZZZ is less impactful than in some other gacha games because early game content is completable with free characters, and the difference between a "good" and "bad" starter account narrows significantly by Month 2. However, a well-rolled account can save you 2-4 weeks of progress toward your first endgame team.

When rerolling IS worth it:

  • You plan to stay completely F2P and want the best possible start with no spending.
  • A specific favorite character is currently on a limited banner and you want to guarantee them.
  • You have 4-8 hours of patience to sit through the tutorial multiple times.
  • You want to minimize the regret factor of pulling a weak standard S-rank (like Nekomiya).

When rerolling is NOT worth it:

  • You plan to spend money (monthly pass + Battle Pass) — the additional pulls offset any bad luck.
  • You hate repeating content and just want to play the game.
  • The current limited banner doesn't feature a top-tier character (e.g., it's a rerun of a niche character).
  • You understand that all standard S-ranks are usable — even the weakest ones clear content fine.

Our recommendation: If you're on the fence, don't reroll. ZZZ gives you enough free pulls in the first week to get a good start regardless of your initial S-rank. The game's power curve is gentle — you won't hit a wall because you missed a specific character. If you're determined to reroll, limit yourself to 10-15 attempts max. Beyond that point, the time investment isn't worth the marginal improvement.

📋 Step-by-Step Reroll Process

Time per reroll attempt: Approximately 25-35 minutes (depending on your reading speed and whether you skip dialogue). Here's the exact process:

  1. Create a new account: Use a spare email address or use the "guest account" feature (if available on your platform). ZZZ does not require a HoYoverse account to start — you can play as guest and link later.
  2. Play through the tutorial: This takes about 15-20 minutes. You'll get your first 3 characters (Anby, Billy, Nicole) and learn basic combat mechanics. Do not skip anything — some pulls are gated behind tutorial completion.
  3. Complete Chapter 1: After the tutorial, you unlock the gacha system. Complete the first story chapter to earn enough Polychromes for approximately 20-30 pulls on the Beginner Banner.
  4. Pull on the Beginner Banner: Spend all your free Master Tapes (blue) on the Beginner Banner (half-price for first 50 pulls). This guarantees at least 1 random S-rank from the standard pool. Record what you got.
  5. Check your S-ranks: You'll get at least 1 standard S-rank from the Beginner Banner guarantee. If you're satisfied, continue playing. If not, go back to step 1.

Optional Advanced Reroll: If you want to maximize further, you can try to get two standard S-ranks. This requires completing more content (Chapter 2, Hollow Zero tutorial, various achievements) for additional pulls. Each attempt takes about 1.5-2 hours but gives you a much stronger start. Only do this if you have significant patience.

🌟 Limited Banner Rerolls

If a top-tier limited banner character is currently available (like Caesar, Burnice, or Yanagi), you can reroll to get them. This is significantly more time-consuming but gives you the best possible start.

The Limited Reroll Process:

  1. Complete the Beginner Banner process (get your standard S-rank).
  2. Continue playing through Chapters 1-2 and all events to accumulate additional Polychromes (approximately 30-40 more pulls).
  3. Pull on the Limited Character Banner. You need to win the 50/50 (50% chance) to get the featured character.
  4. If you lose the 50/50, the account is actually better than average — your next limited S-rank on this account is guaranteed. Decide if you want to keep this account with the standard S-rank + future guarantee, or reroll again.

Current Limited Banner Target Priority (if available):

  • Caesar: The absolute best character to reroll for. She carries your account through all content. Worth 5-10 hours of rerolling.
  • Burnice: Excellent if you plan to play anomaly teams. Worth 3-5 hours.
  • Yanagi: Great but needs a second anomaly character. Worth 2-3 hours if you enjoy Disorder gameplay.
  • Zhu Yuan: Good for burst playstyles. Worth 1-2 hours if you prefer stun teams.

❓ FAQ

Q1: Can I reroll on mobile and then play on PC?

Yes, ZZZ has cross-save across all platforms (PC, mobile, PS5). However, if you're rerolling via guest account, note that guest accounts are device-specific. To ensure cross-platform access, you need to link the account to your HoYoverse email before logging in on a different device. The best workflow: reroll on mobile (faster tutorial skipping) → when satisfied, link to email → play on PC.

Q2: How many free pulls do I get during the tutorial and Chapter 1?

During the tutorial + Chapter 1 completion, you get approximately 20-30 pulls worth of Polychromes and Master Tapes. Specifically: 10 Master Tapes from mail/rewards, 5 from Beginner Banner tutorial, and approximately 800-1,200 Polychromes (5-8 pulls) from chapter completion, achievements, and pre-registration rewards (if applicable). This is not enough to guarantee any specific character — it's enough for your Beginner Banner S-rank guarantee.

Q3: What if I accidentally spent my Polychromes on the wrong banner?

There is no way to undo pulls in ZZZ. If you accidentally spent Polychromes on the Standard Banner or W-Engine Banner as a new player, don't panic — your account isn't ruined. The lost pulls (usually 5-10 if you realize quickly) set you back by about 1-2 weeks. Just save going forward and focus on the Limited Character Banner. Many veteran players have made this mistake and still have strong accounts.

Q4: Is there a faster way to reroll?

On PC, you can speed up the process by: (1) Using an SSD (cuts load times by 60%), (2) Skipping all dialogue (press Space rapidly), (3) Using auto-battle for combat sections, (4) Having multiple browser tabs open for different email accounts. On mobile, the fastest method is to use the "delete account" feature in settings rather than reinstalling the app — this saves 5-10 minutes per attempt. Some players use multi-instance emulators to run 3-4 rerolls simultaneously, cutting effective time per attempt by 60-75%.
