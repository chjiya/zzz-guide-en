🔥 Why Caesar + Burnice?

Caesar King (S-rank Physical Defense) and Burnice (S-rank Fire Anomaly) form one of the strongest core duos in Zenless Zone Zero's current meta. Caesar provides the team with the game's strongest shields (4,000-6,000+ HP at high investment), a passive ATK buff of up to 1,000, and a parry counter that interrupts enemy attacks. Burnice applies constant off-field Fire anomaly buildup through her Afterburn mechanic, enabling Disorder procs without requiring field time.

The synergy between these two is exceptional because Caesar's shield protects the on-field anomaly DPS (usually Piper or Jane Doe) during their long, vulnerable attack animations, while Burnice's off-field damage constantly generates Fire anomaly gauge. When paired with a Physical anomaly agent, the result is consistent Disorder procs that deal massive burst damage (15,000-30,000 per proc depending on investment) while the enemy is kept under pressure.

Core Strengths: Near-permanent shield uptime (Caesar's EX Special generates an 8-second shield with ~12-second cooldown); constant Disorder procs every 6-10 seconds; excellent survivability for squishy anomaly DPS characters; works at both M0 and M1+ levels; no energy-hungry rotations. Weaknesses: Requires specific W-Engine investments on all three slots to reach peak performance; Caesar's personal damage is negligible; Burnice's Afterburn damage falls off without her signature W-Engine; the team has no dedicated stun window — damage is sustained rather than burst.

👑 Premium Disorder Team (Whale)

This is the absolute best team Caesar and Burnice can be in. It requires Yanagi (S-rank Electric Anomaly) as the on-field driver. Yanagi's EX Special triggers Disorder on command, giving you precise control over when Disorder procs happen — a massive improvement over relying on Piper's auto-tick anomaly application.

🆓 F2P Disorder Team

The budget variant replaces Yanagi with Piper (A-rank Physical Anomaly). Piper is obtainable for free from the new player experience and standard banner, making this team accessible to all players. The trade-off is that Disorder procs are less controlled — Piper's anomaly application happens through her hold Basic Attack (the spin), which requires her to be on-field for extended periods.

If you want Burnice to be the main damage dealer rather than a disorder enabler, this team places Burnice on-field and uses Lucy (A-rank Fire Support) as the second support alongside Caesar. This team maximizes Burnice's personal damage through Fire damage bonuses and ATK buffs.

🔄 Rotation Guide

Premium Disorder Rotation (Caesar + Burnice + Yanagi)

  1. Opener (Caesar): Start with Caesar → EX Special Attack to generate team-wide shield (8 seconds) and ATK buff (1,000 ATK for 12 seconds). Immediately swap off.
  2. Burnice Setup: Swap to Burnice → EX Special Attack to apply Afterburn status on the enemy (Fire DoT + anomaly buildup) → Basic Attack until EX energy is spent → swap to Yanagi. Burnice should only be on-field for 2-3 seconds per rotation.
  3. Yanagi Disorder: Yanagi on-field → use Basic Attacks to build Electric anomaly → when you see Fire anomaly gauge is ~70-80% full (from Burnice's Afterburn), use Yanagi's EX Special → this triggers a Disorder proc combining Fire + Electric → Yanagi's passive gives her bonus Energy for future EX Specials.
  4. Loop: Repeat steps 1-3. Caesar's shield should be refreshed every 10-12 seconds. Burnice's Afterburn lasts 10 seconds — reapply when it drops off. Yanagi stays on-field for 5-8 seconds per cycle.

F2P Disorder Rotation (Caesar + Burnice + Piper)

  1. Opener (Caesar): Same as premium — EX Special for shield and ATK buff.
  2. Burnice Setup: Same as premium — EX Special to apply Afterburn, then swap off.
  3. Piper Spin: Piper on-field → hold Basic Attack to begin spinning → continue spinning for 3-4 seconds until Physical anomaly gauge fills → anomaly procs (Assault) → this triggers Disorder with Burnice's Fire anomaly → Piper's Assault deals around 15,000-25,000 damage and also does the Disorder burst.
  4. Loop: Same shield refresh cadence. Piper needs to spin for the full duration — do not cancel her spin early as this dramatically reduces DPS.

❓ FAQ

Q1: Can I use Lucy instead of Caesar in the F2P team?

You can, but it's not recommended. Lucy provides a 600 ATK buff (at M6) and summonable guards that do decent damage, but she offers zero survivability. Piper needs the shield from Caesar to safely execute her full spin animation. Without Caesar's shield, Piper will be interrupted by enemy attacks frequently, reducing your actual DPS by 30-40%. If you must use Lucy, pair her with a stunner like Anby to give Piper safer windows.

Q2: Is Caesar or Burnice more important to pull first?

Caesar is the higher priority pull because she is universally useful across every team in the game. Burnice is stronger within Disorder-specific teams but has fewer use cases outside of anomaly compositions. If you can only get one, get Caesar. If you have both, the team is among the strongest in ZZZ's current meta.

Q3: What mindscapes are worth getting for this team?

Burnice M1 (increases Energy Regen by 25% when Afterburn is active, enabling more EX Specials) is the most impactful M1 for this specific team. Caesar M1 (increases shield duration by 3 seconds) is nice but not essential — the base 8 seconds is sufficient if you manage energy well. Yanagi M2 (increases Disorder damage by 35%) is a massive damage spike for the premium team but unnecessary for F2P builds. None of these characters need mindscapes to function — they all perform well at M0.

Q4: What if I don't have Caesar or Burnice?

If you're missing Caesar, replace her with Seth (A-rank Electric Defense) who provides anomaly proficiency buff — this reduces Piper's spin time by about 1 second. If you're missing Burnice, replace her with Lucy (A-rank Fire Support) — you lose Disorder but gain a mono-Physical team that still clears content effectively. If you're missing both, consider running Piper + Seth + Lucy as a budget anomaly team until you can pull Caesar and/or Burnice.
