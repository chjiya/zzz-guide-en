Hollow Zero Complete Guide 🕳️

📌 v1.0 • Updated June 4, 2026 • Roguelike • Endgame • Weekly

Overview

Hollow Zero is Zenless Zone Zero's flagship roguelike game mode — a sprawling, procedurally generated dungeon crawl set deep within the most dangerous Hollows of New Eridu. Unlike standard combat missions, Hollow Zero challenges players to navigate through interconnected zones filled with randomized enemy encounters, corruption events, and powerful Resonium (roquelike buffs) that dramatically alter your team's capabilities.

Hollow Zero is the primary source of Investigation Merits — a weekly-capped currency used to purchase Agent Talent materials, W-Engine upgrade components, and exclusive items from the Outpost Logistics shop. It is also the best place to earn Agent EXP materials, Dennies, and upgrade mats in bulk. Every player should aim to cap their weekly Investigation Merits for maximum account progression.

What makes Hollow Zero unique is the Corruption mechanic. As you progress deeper into a run, your agents accumulate Corruption, which applies stacking debuffs such as reduced healing, increased damage taken, or disabled Perfect Assists. Managing Corruption through strategic routing, event choices, and Resonium selection is the key to consistently clearing high-difficulty Hollow Zero runs.

How to Unlock & Access

Hollow Zero unlocks after completing Chapter 1 of the main story and reaching Inter-Knot Level 20. Once unlocked, you can access it via the HDD system from the main menu or by talking to Ray at the Scott Outpost hub area.

The mode offers multiple difficulty tiers that unlock as your Inter-Knot Level increases:

Core Mechanics: Corruption & Pressure

Understanding Hollow Zero's unique mechanics is essential for consistent clears:

Corruption

Corruption is a stacking debuff that increases as you clear zones. Each zone completion adds a base amount of Corruption, and certain events or enemy attacks can spike your Corruption further. At each Corruption threshold, your team receives a negative effect:

  • Low (0-30): No debuffs. Safe to fight anything.
  • Moderate (31-60): Healing reduced by 30%. Enemies deal 15% more damage.
  • High (61-90): Healing reduced by 60%. Perfect Assist window shortened. Agent-swap cooldown increased.
  • Critical (91+): Agents lose HP over time. All debuffs doubled. Avoid reaching this threshold at all costs.

Pressure

Pressure is your team's resistance to Corruption. Certain Resonium types (particularly Defense and Support category Resonium) increase Pressure, reducing the Corruption you accumulate per zone. A balanced Resonium build with 2-3 Pressure-boosting cards can keep your Corruption comfortably in the Low-to-Moderate range throughout the entire run.

Resonium System

Resonium are the heart of Hollow Zero's roguelike experience — collectible buff cards that provide permanent stat boosts, unique combat effects, and team synergies throughout your run. Resonium come in five categories:

CategoryFocusBest For
AttackATK%, CRIT, DMG amplificationDPS-focused teams; universally strong.
StunDaze buildup, Stun duration, Chain Attack DMGStun-centric comps; enables faster burst windows.
DefenseShields, Damage Reduction, PressureSustainability; reduces Corruption accumulation.
SupportEnergy Regen, Healing, buff sharingTeams with dedicated Support agents.
AnomalyAnomaly Buildup, Disorder DMG, Attribute-specific buffsAnomaly teams (Jane Doe, Grace, Piper).

Resonium Strategy

Focus on a core synergy rather than spreading Resonium across categories. A typical successful run collects 8-12 Resonium cards. Prioritize picking Resonium that match your team's primary damage type and playstyle:

  • Attack teams: Stack Attack Resonium. Look for "Crimson Blade" and "Thunderous Roar" series for DMG% stacking.
  • Anomaly teams: Stack Anomaly Resonium. "Ethereal Bloom" and "Chaotic Flux" series boost Disorder damage dramatically.
  • Every team: Grab at least 1-2 Defense Resonium to manage Pressure and keep Corruption low.

Zone Types & Event Choices

Each zone in Hollow Zero is represented by a node on the map. The node's icon tells you what to expect:

Node TypeIconWhat to Expect
Combat⚔️ Crossed SwordsStandard enemy wave. Rewards Resonium and currency. Lowest risk.
Elite Combat💀 SkullElite enemy or mini-boss. Higher Resonium rewards but more Corruption gain.
Event❓ Question MarkRandom narrative event. Can grant powerful buffs, Resonium, or reduce Corruption — but can also backfire.
Rest🏕️ CampfireHeals your team and reduces Corruption. Always take these when available.
Shop🛒 CartSpend earned currency on Resonium or healing. Visit when you have spare coins.
Boss👁️ EyeFinal zone boss. Exclusive to Core difficulty and above.

Routing tip: Prioritize Rest nodes when your Corruption is Moderate or higher. Favor Event nodes early in a run when the stakes are lower — Events can grant some of the strongest Resonium in the game. Avoid Elite Combat nodes if your team is already struggling or your Corruption is approaching 60.

General Composition Rules

  • Always bring a Stun agent. Daze buildup is essential for creating safe damage windows against elites and bosses.
  • Bring at least one form of sustain: a Support with healing, a Defense agent with shields, or enough Defense Resonium to manage Corruption.
  • Avoid triple-DPS comps. Without dedicated Stun or Support, you'll struggle with Corruption management and elite enemies.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is Hollow Zero in Zenless Zone Zero?

Hollow Zero is Zenless Zone Zero's roguelike game mode where players navigate through procedurally generated zones filled with enemies, events, and bosses. It is the primary source of weekly Investigation Merits, Agent Talent materials, and Resonium farming. Each run is different due to randomized maps, events, and Resonium drops.

Q: What is the best team for Hollow Zero?

The best teams for Hollow Zero are AoE-focused comps that can clear multiple waves efficiently. Agents like Zhu Yuan (Ether Attack), Ellen Joe (Ice Attack), and Soldier 11 (Fire Attack) excel. Always bring a Stun agent and a Support or Defense agent for survivability. Jane Doe-based Anomaly teams are also extremely effective due to how well they synergize with Anomaly Resonium.

Q: How many times can I run Hollow Zero per week?

Hollow Zero weekly rewards are capped at a maximum amount of Investigation Merits. You can run Hollow Zero as many times as you want for practice, but the reward cap resets every Monday (server time). Most players need 2-3 full runs of Core difficulty or 1-2 runs of Withering Garden to cap weekly rewards.

Q: How do I reduce Corruption in Hollow Zero?

Reduce Corruption by: (1) visiting Rest nodes on the map, (2) selecting event choices that cleanse Corruption, (3) equipping Defense-category Resonium that increases Pressure, (4) avoiding Elite Combat nodes when your Corruption is already high. Keeping Corruption below 60 is the goal for a comfortable run.

Q: What are the best Resonium categories to pick?

Attack category Resonium is universally strong and should be your default priority. If running an Anomaly team, Anomaly Resonium becomes your top pick. Always grab at least 1-2 Defense Resonium for Pressure management. Avoid heavily mixing categories — focus on one primary synergy for the best results.
