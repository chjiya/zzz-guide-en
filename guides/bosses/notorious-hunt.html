Notorious Hunt Guide 💀

📌 v1.0 • Updated June 4, 2026 • Weekly Boss • Talent Materials • Endgame

Overview

Notorious Hunt is Zenless Zone Zero's weekly boss challenge mode, accessible after reaching Inter-Knot Level 30 and completing the main story through Chapter 2. Each week, you receive three free hunt attempts to challenge powerful boss variants — Dead End Butcher, Unknown Corruption Complex, Nineveh (Hollow Zero final boss), and others — for rare Talent upgrade materials that are essential for leveling Agent core skills beyond level 6.

Unlike standard boss encounters, Notorious Hunt bosses have enhanced move sets, larger health pools, and deal significantly more damage. They drop unique weekly materials (such as "Ferocious Grip" from the Dead End Butcher or "Core Overload Module" from the Unknown Corruption Complex) that cannot be obtained anywhere else. These materials are gated behind weekly attempts, making Notorious Hunt one of the most important weekly activities for account progression.

Boss Strategies

Dead End Butcher

Element: Physical | Weakness: Ether, Ice

The Dead End Butcher is a relentless melee boss with devastating chain combos and a berserk phase below 50% HP. Its attacks are heavily telegraphed but hit in wide arcs, making positioning critical.

  • Key attack — Guillotine Slam: The Butcher leaps into the air and crashes down with its blade. Dodge sideways, not backward, as the attack has a forward shockwave.
  • Berserk phase (below 50% HP): Attack speed increases dramatically and the Butcher gains a damage resistance buff. Save your Ultimate and Chain Attacks for this phase to burst through quickly.
  • Parry window: The Butcher's 3-hit combo has a generous Perfect Assist window on the second swing. Parry this to trigger a Chain Attack and interrupt the combo.
  • Recommended team: Zhu Yuan + Anby + Nicole. Ether weakness and Zhu Yuan's burst windows align perfectly with the Butcher's post-attack recovery frames.

Unknown Corruption Complex

Element: Ether | Weakness: Fire, Electric

The Unknown Corruption Complex is a floating mechanical boss that attacks with energy orbs, lasers, and summons corrupted minions. It has a unique shield phase that must be broken with Daze damage.

  • Key attack — Corruption Beam: Charges a wide horizontal laser that sweeps the arena. Dodge through the beam (i-frame timing) or use a Perfect Assist with a Defense agent to block it.
  • Shield Phase (periodic): The Complex generates an Ether shield that absorbs damage. Focus on Daze buildup with your Stun agent — breaking the shield stuns the boss for an extended window.
  • Summoned minions: Corrupted drones spawn periodically. Ignore them and focus on the boss, but use AoE abilities to clear them when they cluster.
  • Recommended team: Soldier 11 + Koleda + Ben. Fire weakness exploitation with Koleda's fast Daze buildup for shield phases.

Nineveh (Hollow Zero Boss)

Element: Ether | Weakness: Ice, Physical

Nineveh is a massive serpentine Ethereal that serves as Hollow Zero's final boss and appears in Notorious Hunt at higher difficulty levels. Its attacks cover large portions of the arena and it frequently repositions.

  • Key attack — Ethereal Breath: Nineveh spews a cone of Ether energy that lingers as a damaging zone. Move to the sides immediately — the center of the arena becomes dangerous for several seconds.
  • Tail Sweep: A 360-degree tail attack that hits twice. Dodge the first sweep and immediately prepare for the follow-up. The second hit has delayed timing.
  • Burrow phase: Nineveh dives underground and erupts at a new location. Watch for the ground ripple to predict where it will surface, and have your Stun agent ready to start Daze buildup immediately.
  • Recommended team: Ellen Joe + Lycaon + Soukaku. Ice weakness combined with Lycaon's Ice RES shred and Soukaku's ATK buffs creates devastating burst windows.

Weekly Optimization

  1. Always spend your 3 free attempts every week. The materials are time-gated and cannot be farmed elsewhere. Missing a week means permanently falling behind on Talent upgrades.
  2. Run the highest difficulty you can clear comfortably. Higher difficulties (III and IV) drop 2-3 materials per run instead of 1-2, dramatically improving efficiency. Aim to unlock Difficulty IV (IK 45+) as soon as possible.
  3. Plan around your agent roster. Before the Monday reset, check which agents you plan to build in the coming week and pre-farm their corresponding boss materials.
  4. Solo is faster than co-op. While co-op is available, solo runs with your own teams are generally quicker because you control all three agents' rotations and can optimize burst timing perfectly.
  5. Save attempts until after weekly reset activities. If you have Battle Pass missions tied to Notorious Hunt completions, coordinate your runs to complete both simultaneously.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is Notorious Hunt in Zenless Zone Zero?

Notorious Hunt is the weekly boss mode in Zenless Zone Zero where players challenge powerful boss variants for rare Talent upgrade materials. You get three free hunt attempts per week, and each boss drops unique materials needed to level core Agent skills past certain thresholds. The mode unlocks at Inter-Knot Level 30 after completing Chapter 2 of the main story.

Q: How many times can I do Notorious Hunt per week?

You receive three free Notorious Hunt attempts per week, which reset every Monday (server time). Each attempt costs 60 Battery Charge (Stamina). You can also use extra attempts by spending Battery Charge beyond the three free ones, but this is generally not recommended as the free attempts cover most material needs.

Q: Which Notorious Hunt boss should I farm first?

Farm the boss that drops materials for your current priority agents. Check your agents' Talent upgrade requirements and match them to the boss drop table. Generally, the Dead End Butcher and Unknown Corruption Complex are farmed most frequently as their materials are needed by a wide range of popular agents across multiple factions.

Q: What difficulty level should I run?

Always run the highest difficulty you can clear within a reasonable time (under 5 minutes). Difficulty IV drops the most materials per run and is the most Battery-efficient. If Difficulty IV is too challenging, Difficulty III still provides good returns. Avoid Difficulty I and II once you have level 50+ agents.

Q: Do I need to spend all 3 attempts on the same boss?

Not necessarily. You can split your three weekly attempts across different bosses if you're building agents that require different materials. However, focusing on one boss until you've stockpiled enough materials for your current roster is more efficient than spreading attempts thin.
