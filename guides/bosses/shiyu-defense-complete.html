🏛️ What is Shiyu Defense?

Shiyu Defense is the primary endgame activity in Zenless Zone Zero. It is a time-limited DPS check that resets every two weeks and requires two teams of three agents each. It has two components: the Stable Node (floors 1-7, fixed content that doesn't reset) and the Critical Node (floors 5-7, rotating content with different enemy lineups and buffs each season).

Clearing Shiyu Defense is essential for F2P players because it's the largest recurring source of Polychromes (940-1,440 per month at S-rank clears). It also provides exclusive name cards, titles, and achievement badges for milestone completions.

Entry Requirements: Proxy Level 40+ (unlocked after completing Chapter 3 of the main story). You need two teams of at least level 50 characters with level 40+ W-Engines for the lower floors. For Critical Node S-rank clears, level 55-60 characters with level 50+ W-Engines are recommended.

🏗️ Stable Node Guide (Floors 1-7)

The Stable Node is permanent content. Each floor has fixed enemies and a fixed buff. You only need to clear it once — the rewards do not reset. However, you can challenge higher-difficulty versions of Stable floors for one-time Polychrome rewards up to floor 15 in the "Stable Corridor" expansion.

🔄 Critical Node Guide (Rotating Content)

The Critical Node is the real challenge. It resets every 2 weeks with different enemy lineups and a rotating buff called the Stage Effect. Each season features different enemy types on floors 5, 6, and 7 — you must clear all three with two separate teams.

How the Stage Effect Works: Each season has a primary mechanic that boosts certain playstyles. For example, Season 1 had "Increased Disorder DMG by 50%," which heavily favored anomaly teams. Season 2 had "Increased Crit DMG during Stun by 75%," favoring burst teams. Always build your teams around the current Stage Effect for easier clears.

Critical Node Difficulty: The enemies on Critical Node floors 5-7 are level 60-70 with significantly inflated HP pools (100,000-300,000 HP each). The S-rank time limit is typically 180-300 seconds per floor depending on the season's difficulty modifier. More difficult seasons have longer time limits to compensate.

👹 Boss Mechanics & Counters

Shadow of Marrionette (Ether Floor 7)

Mechanics: Phase 1 — Puppet attacks with wide sweeping combos. Phase 2 — summons 2 clones that mimic its attacks. The real boss has a purple aura.

Counter: Use Caesar's parry to interrupt the sweeping combo. In Phase 2, ignore the clones and focus the real one (purple aura). AoE characters like Burnice (Afterburn hits all enemies) are excellent here.

Notorious Pompeii (Fire Boss)

Mechanics: Has a Fire shield that reduces all damage by 50% until broken. Uses a heat-seeking charge attack that leaves a fire trail.

Counter: Break the shield with Electric or Physical attacks (Anby or Piper are great). The charge attack can be parried with perfect timing. Avoid fighting Pompeii with a Fire team — 50% damage reduction is crippling.

Sacred Mauler (Physical Boss, Common in Critical Node)

Mechanics: Has a rage mechanic — below 30% HP, its attack speed doubles and it gains a 25% DMG increase. Also has a roar attack that stuns your active character for 2 seconds.

Counter: Save your chain attacks and ultimates for the sub-30% phase. The roar attack can be dodged (dodge backward at the flash). Caesar's shield is invaluable here to survive the rage phase damage spike.

Nineveh (Ether Boss, Hollow Zero Final Boss)

Mechanics: Teleports frequently, spawns ether explosions on the ground, and has a massive AoE ultimate that covers 80% of the arena.

Counter: Use ranged characters (Nicole, Zhu Yuan) to deal damage during teleport phases. The AoE ultimate can be dodged by staying at the edge of the arena (the center blast zone is the dangerous part). Bring a stunner to interrupt the teleport chain.

General Critical Node Tips

  • Check the enemy elements: Each floor tells you which element the enemies are weak to. Match your DPS element for 25% more damage.
  • Redo floors for better RNG: If you barely miss S-rank (within 5 seconds), you can retry. Boss attack patterns are somewhat random — a run with fewer dodges and more attack windows can save you the needed time.
  • Two-team strategy: Put your stronger team on the harder floor (usually floor 7 has the tankiest boss). Your weaker team should tackle floor 6, which typically has a simpler enemy lineup.
  • Food buffs: Use the food buff system before entering. The +25% ATK food (Satisfying Fish Noodle Soup) is the most generally useful. For anomaly teams, the +30 Anomaly Proficiency food is better.

⭐ S-Rank Strategy & Tips

  1. Know the timer: The S-rank time limit is clearly shown before you enter. The typical limit is 180 seconds (3 minutes) for floor 7 and 240 seconds for floors 5-6. Divide total HP by time to find your DPS target — roughly 1,000-1,500 DPS per character for S-rank.
  2. Optimize rotations: Don't waste time on characters who aren't contributing. If your stunner has done their job (stun applied), swap to your DPS immediately. Every second outside stun is a second of reduced damage for burst teams.
  3. Use the pause trick: You can pause the game (Escape key) to check remaining time and enemy HP without the timer running. Use this to decide if you need to play more aggressively or if you can afford to play safe.
  4. Prioritize floor 5 for S-rank: Floor 5 is usually the easiest, so focus on getting a fast clear there first. If you get S-rank on floors 5 and 6 but only A-rank on floor 7, your overall rating is A. Try swapping which team goes to which floor.
  5. Don't die: Losing a character costs you 30+ seconds of DPS while your backup character loads in and builds energy. If you're about to take lethal damage, swap characters immediately (the incoming character gets brief invincibility frames).

❓ FAQ

Q1: What level should my characters be for Shiyu Defense Critical Node?

For the Critical Node floors 5-7, you want your main DPS at level 55-60, your supports at level 50+, and their W-Engines at level 50-60. The most important factor is the Drive Disc quality — even level 50 characters with good Drive Discs (correct main stats, +9-12 enhancement) can clear Critical Node. Level 60 with bad Drive Discs will struggle. Prioritize leveling Drive Discs over character levels after level 55.

Q2: Can I clear Shiyu Defense with A-rank characters only?

Yes, you can clear Shiyu Defense with only A-rank characters, including S-rank clears on most rotations. The best A-rank team is Piper (M6) + Lucy (M6) + Anby (M6). At full investment (level 60, +15 Drive Discs, R5 W-Engines), this team can clear Critical Node floors 5-6 in 120-150 seconds. Floor 7 may require a reset or two if the boss is unfriendly to your composition. This team clears significantly slower than S-rank teams but comfortably hits the S-rank time threshold.

Q3: What's the best way to practice for Shiyu Defense?

The best practice is Hollow Zero (high difficulty) and Notorious Hunt. Hollow Zero teaches you sustained DPS rotation management across multiple fights. Notorious Hunt teaches you boss-specific mechanics. Both modes have no time pressure, so you can focus on learning attack patterns and optimizing your rotation. Once you can clear Notorious Hunt level 5+ consistently, you're ready for Shiyu Defense Critical Node.

Q4: Does Shiyu Defense get harder every season?

Yes and no. The base difficulty has a slight upward trend as character power increases (new characters and W-Engines push the ceiling higher). However, the Stage Effect mechanic means some seasons are significantly easier than others depending on your account. A season with "Increased Disorder DMG" is easy if you have Yanagi + Burnice but hard if you only have stun characters. We recommend building at least one team from each archetype (anomaly, stun) so you can adapt to any season's buff.
