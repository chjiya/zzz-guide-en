Shiyu Defense Guide ⚔️

📌 v1.0 • Updated June 4, 2026 • Endgame • Bi-Weekly • S-Rank

Overview

Shiyu Defense is Zenless Zone Zero's premier endgame challenge — a multi-floor combat gauntlet that tests your account's depth, team-building skills, and mechanical execution. Unlike standard missions, Shiyu Defense demands two fully independent teams for its later floors, meaning you cannot lean on a single hyper-invested carry to clear everything.

Shiyu Defense is split into two major sections: Stable Node and Critical Node. Stable Node (floors 1-7) serves as a permanent, one-time-clear progression track with fixed enemy lineups and generous rewards. Critical Node (floors 8-10) is the rotating bi-weekly content that veteran players grind for Polychromes, premium currency, and bragging rights. An additional Ambush Node sometimes appears as an even harder challenge with unique modifiers.

Mastering Shiyu Defense is the key to sustaining a F2P Polychrome income. Each full Critical Node clear awards up to 720 Polychromes per cycle, which adds up to approximately 1,440 Polychromes per month — nearly a 10-pull every patch cycle just from this mode alone.

Critical Node Floor Breakdown

Each Critical Node floor is split into two halves (Frontline and Backline), each requiring a separate team. Here's what to expect at each floor:

Floor 8 — The Gatekeeper

  • Enemy Level: 60-65
  • Difficulty: Moderate. The entry point to bi-weekly rewards.
  • Frontline: Usually 2-3 elite enemies with mob waves. AoE-capable teams excel here.
  • Backline: Often features a single high-HP elite or mini-boss. Single-target DPS preferred.
  • S-Rank Threshold: ~4-5 minutes combined. Achievable with well-built but not hyper-invested teams.

Floor 9 — The DPS Check

  • Enemy Level: 65-70
  • Difficulty: Hard. This is where team investment starts to matter.
  • Frontline: Often features enemies with elemental shields (Ether, Ice, or Fire). Match your DPS element to the enemy weakness.
  • Backline: Features one of the weekly boss variants or a high-threat elite like Thanatos. Bring a Stun agent with fast Daze buildup.
  • S-Rank Threshold: ~5 minutes combined. Requires level 50+ agents with decent Drive Discs and W-Engines.

Floor 10 — The Final Gauntlet

  • Enemy Level: 70+
  • Difficulty: Very Hard. The ultimate test of your account.
  • Frontline: Dense elite waves with overlapping attack patterns. Defense agents or shielded supports are valuable.
  • Backline: Features the cycle's featured boss with enhanced stats and sometimes unique floor mechanics.
  • S-Rank Threshold: ~5-6 minutes combined. Demands level 60 agents with optimized Drive Discs, leveled talents, and maxed W-Engines.

Team Building Strategy

Since you cannot reuse agents between halves of the same floor, your roster must support two complete teams. Here's how to allocate your resources:

Elemental Matchup Priority

Each Shiyu Defense cycle features enemies with specific elemental weaknesses. Check the Enemy Intel panel before each floor to see which elements are favored. Prioritize sending your strongest team to the half with the tougher matchup:

  • Favored elements deal 20% more damage and build Daze 20% faster.
  • Resisted elements deal 20% less damage. Avoid sending resisted-element DPS agents into floors where enemies resist their type.
  • Neutral elements (no advantage or disadvantage) are perfectly fine — raw stats and team synergy matter more than elemental matching at high investment.

Floor Buffs & Cycle Mechanics

Each Shiyu Defense cycle introduces a Cycle Buff — a unique combat modifier that applies across all Critical Node floors. These buffs rotate every two weeks and heavily influence which team compositions perform best:

  • Chain Attack Bonus: Chain Attacks deal 50% increased damage. Favors Stun-heavy comps with frequent Chain Attack windows.
  • Ultimate Accelerator: Ultimates charge 30% faster. Favors agents with high-impact Ultimates like Ellen Joe and Zhu Yuan.
  • Anomaly Amplifier: Attribute Anomaly damage increased by 40%. Anomaly teams (Jane Doe, Grace, Piper) dominate these cycles.
  • EX Special Surge: EX Special Attacks cost 20% less Energy. Benefits agents with powerful EX Specials like Miyabi and Soldier 11.

Always read the cycle buff description before finalizing your teams. Building around the buff can make the difference between an A-Rank and S-Rank clear.

Scoring & S-Rank Thresholds

Shiyu Defense scores are based on clear time. The faster you clear, the higher your rank:

Tips for S-Rank clears:

  1. Restart a half if you lose an agent early — a death is almost always a reset.
  2. Group enemies together before using AoE abilities. Perfect Assists are great for clustering.
  3. Use Ultimates and Chain Attacks during Stun windows for maximum burst efficiency.
  4. On Floor 10, consider sending your weaker team to the Frontline (which usually has a more generous time limit) and your strongest team to the Backline boss.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is Shiyu Defense in Zenless Zone Zero?

Shiyu Defense is Zenless Zone Zero's primary endgame mode — a rotating bi-weekly challenge consisting of multiple floors of escalating difficulty. It requires two fully-built teams and rewards Polychromes, Dennies, and upgrade materials based on clear speed and completion rank.

Q: How many teams do I need for Shiyu Defense?

You need two separate teams for Shiyu Defense's Critical Node (floors 8-10) and Ambush Node, as each floor is split into two halves. Agents cannot be shared between halves within the same floor, so you must build at least 6 agents across two full teams of 3 agents each.

Q: How often does Shiyu Defense reset?

Shiyu Defense resets every two weeks (bi-weekly), typically on a Wednesday or Thursday depending on your server. Each reset refreshes all rewards, shuffles enemy lineups, and may introduce new floor buffs or cycle mechanics. The Stable Node (floors 1-7) never resets — it's a one-time clear.

Q: What Inter-Knot level do I need for Shiyu Defense?

Shiyu Defense unlocks at Inter-Knot Level 25 for the Stable Node (floors 1-3). Higher floors unlock progressively: Floor 4-5 at IK 30, Floor 6-7 at IK 35, and Critical Node (floors 8-10) at IK 40 or above. Realistically, you'll want IK 45+ with level 50+ agents before seriously attempting S-Rank Critical Node clears.

Q: Is Shiyu Defense worth doing every cycle?

Absolutely. It's the single most reliable source of recurring Polychromes in the game. Even if you can only clear Floor 8, that's still 240 Polychromes every two weeks. As your account grows, each additional floor you clear adds more to your income. It's also the best test of whether your builds and team compositions are working.
