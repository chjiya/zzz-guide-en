Weekly Boss Complete Guide 👾

📌 v1.0 • Updated June 4, 2026 • Endgame • Weekly Reset

Weekly Boss System Overview

Weekly bosses are the cornerstone of ZZZ's endgame progression loop. These powerful Ethereal enemies drop essential character ascension materials that are required to level agents past certain thresholds, as well as high-quality Drive Discs and substantial amounts of Dennies. Each week, you can claim rewards from up to three boss encounters, and the selection of available bosses expands as you progress through the story and raise your Inter-Knot level.

Unlike standard combat encounters, weekly bosses feature complex multi-phase attack patterns, high HP pools, and powerful enrage mechanics that punish unprepared teams. Understanding each boss's attack telegraphs, elemental weaknesses, and optimal DPS windows is essential for consistent clears — especially at higher difficulty levels where enemy damage can one-shot under-leveled agents.

This guide covers every weekly boss currently available, including their attack patterns, recommended team compositions, elemental weaknesses and resistances, and strategies for both normal farming and challenge-mode clears. Whether you are a new player facing your first weekly boss or a veteran optimizing your weekly farming routine, this guide will help you clear these encounters efficiently and consistently.

General Tips for All Weekly Bosses

  • Bring the Right Element: Every weekly boss has specific elemental weaknesses and resistances. Attacking with a resisted element deals 40% less damage, while exploiting a weakness deals bonus damage and builds Daze faster.
  • Level Your Agents: Weekly bosses have recommended Inter-Knot levels. Fighting them under-leveled results in damage penalties and increased incoming damage. Aim to be within 5 levels of the boss's level.
  • Learn the Parry Windows: Most boss attacks have visible telegraphs — glowing indicators, distinct audio cues, or windup animations. Perfect Dodges and Perfect Assists (parries) not only avoid damage but also apply significant Daze to the boss.
  • Save Ultimates for Stun Windows: Bosses take 150% increased damage during Stun. Always hold your Ultimate for these windows unless you need the i-frames to survive a dangerous attack pattern.
  • Weekly Reset is Monday 4:00 AM Server Time: Plan your boss runs around the reset to maximize your weekly material income. Running bosses early in the week gives you materials for the rest of your farming.

Dead End Butcher

AttributeDetails
WeaknessIce, Ether
ResistancePhysical
Recommended LevelInter-Knot Level 30+ (Normal), 40+ (Hard)
DropsButcher's Core (ascension material), Polar Metal Drive Discs, Dennies

Attack Patterns

The Dead End Butcher is a massive melee-range Ethereal that uses sweeping arm attacks and ground-pound AoEs. It has two distinct phases:

Phase 1 (100% - 60% HP): The Butcher uses slow, telegraphed single-arm sweeps that are easy to Perfect Dodge. Its most dangerous attack in this phase is a three-hit combo: a horizontal sweep → an overhead slam → a spinning double-arm sweep. The second and third hits have generous dodge windows, but taking the first hit can stagger-lock you into the full combo. Stay close to the Butcher during Phase 1 — its attacks have blind spots directly beneath its body where melee agents can safely DPS.

Phase 2 (60% - 0% HP): At 60% HP, the Butcher enrages. Its skin turns crimson and it gains two new attacks: a charging rush that crosses the entire arena (dodge sideways, not backward) and a ground-pound that creates expanding shockwaves in four directions (dodge into the gaps between shockwaves). The Butcher also summons smaller Ethereal adds that swarm you while the boss continues attacking. Eliminate the adds quickly — they deal surprising damage and can interrupt your DPS rotation.

Recommended Teams

  • Best: Miyabi + Lycaon + Soukaku (Mono-Ice, exploiting Ice weakness)
  • Strong: Zhu Yuan + Qingyi + Nicole (Ether weakness, ranged safety advantage)
  • Budget: Ellen Joe + Anby + Soukaku (Ellen is strong against Ice-weak bosses even without Lycaon)

Strategy

During Phase 1, play aggressively. The Butcher's attacks are slow and easily dodged, so you can maintain near-100% DPS uptime. Build Daze with your Stun agent and save your Support buffs for the first Stun window. In Phase 2, the key is managing adds — use AoE abilities (Miyabi's charged attack, Zhu Yuan's EX Special) to clear them while damaging the boss. The Butcher's charging rush is the most lethal Phase 2 attack; always keep enough stamina for an emergency dodge. When you see it plant both arms into the ground (shockwave telegraph), immediately create distance and look for safe zones between shockwave lines.

Nineveh, the Corrupted

AttributeDetails
WeaknessFire, Electric
ResistanceIce
Recommended LevelInter-Knot Level 35+ (Normal), 45+ (Hard)
DropsCorrupted Core (ascension material), Inferno Metal Drive Discs, Dennies

Attack Patterns

Nineveh is a flying Ethereal boss that specializes in ranged projectile attacks and area denial mechanics. Unlike the Butcher, Nineveh spends significant time airborne and out of melee reach, making ranged DPS agents particularly valuable here.

Phase 1 (100% - 70% HP): Nineveh hovers at mid-range and fires volleys of homing corruption orbs (3 waves of 4 orbs each). These orbs track your position and can be dodged by sprinting perpendicular to their trajectory. Between volleys, Nineveh descends briefly for a melee-range claw swipe — this is your primary DPS window. After each claw swipe, it immediately ascends and resumes the orb volley pattern.

Phase 2 (70% - 0% HP): Nineveh gains a corruption aura that periodically drops corruption pools on the arena floor. Standing in a pool deals continuous damage and applies a stacking DEF debuff. The boss also gains a laser-beam attack: Nineveh charges energy for 2 seconds, then fires a continuous sweeping beam across 180 degrees. The beam is lethal at close range — either dodge behind Nineveh or sprint to the far side of the arena. At 30% HP, Nineveh enters a desperation phase where it fires corruption orbs and laser beams simultaneously, demanding precise positioning.

Recommended Teams

  • Best: Soldier 11 + Koleda + Lucy (Mono-Fire, exploits Fire weakness and Sustained damage)
  • Strong: Grace + Piper + Rina (Disorder team; Shock procs exploit Electric weakness)
  • Budget: Billy + Anby + Nicole (Billy's ranged Basic attacks maintain uptime while Nineveh hovers)

Strategy

Nineveh demands patience and positioning. During the orb volley phase, focus purely on dodging — do not greed for damage. The DPS window opens when Nineveh descends for its claw swipe; use this 3-4 second window to dump EX Specials and build Daze. Corruption pools in Phase 2 force constant repositioning; always move to clean floor after each pool drop. The laser beam's 2-second charge telegraph is your signal to reposition behind Nineveh. Ranged DPS agents (Zhu Yuan, Billy) have a natural advantage here since they can damage Nineveh even during its hovering phases.

Unknown Corruption Complex

AttributeDetails
WeaknessElectric, Ether
ResistanceFire
Recommended LevelInter-Knot Level 40+ (Normal), 50+ (Hard)
DropsComplex Core (ascension material), Chaotic Metal Drive Discs, Dennies

Attack Patterns

The Unknown Corruption Complex is the most mechanically demanding weekly boss. It is a modular Ethereal composed of multiple floating segments that attack independently, creating multi-directional threat patterns that test your spatial awareness and dodge timing.

Phase 1 (100% - 50% HP): The Complex attacks with three segments simultaneously: a core orb that fires targeted energy bolts, a blade arm that performs sweeping melee strikes, and a shield arm that periodically makes the boss immune to damage from the front. The key to Phase 1 is positioning behind the Complex — the shield only blocks frontal attacks, and the blade arm's sweeps have a blind spot directly behind the core. The energy bolts are fired in patterns of 3, then 5, then 7, and can be dodged by tapping dodge rhythmically.

Phase 2 (50% - 0% HP): At half HP, the Complex combines its segments into a giant humanoid form. It gains a devastating grab attack (telegraphed by a glowing red hand) that deals massive damage and applies Corrosion. The combined form also performs a three-hit arena-wide slam combo. Perfect Assist (parry) is the optimal response to the slam combo — each successful parry applies massive Daze to the boss. At 20% HP, the Complex enters a final rage where all attacks become faster and deal 30% increased damage.

Recommended Teams

  • Best: Zhu Yuan + Qingyi + Nicole (Ether weakness, Qingyi's fast Stun for frequent burst windows)
  • Strong: Grace + Anby + Rina (Electric weakness, Anby's parry-centric kit excels against Phase 2 slams)
  • Budget: Corin + Anby + Nicole (Physical damage works neutrally; Anby and Nicole are free)

Strategy

Phase 1 is about positioning: always move to the Complex's rear to bypass the shield. Use your Stun agent to build Daze while circling. When the energy bolt pattern escalates to 7 bolts, stop attacking and focus entirely on dodging — the cumulative damage of multiple bolt hits can kill an agent from full HP. In Phase 2, parrying the slam combo is the most efficient way to build Daze. The grab attack has a distinctive audio cue (a rising mechanical whine) — when you hear it, immediately dodge backward twice. During the final 20% rage phase, use Ultimates defensively if needed — their i-frames can save you from lethal attack overlaps.

Weekly Boss Reward Optimization

Weekly boss rewards are claimable three times per week. Here is how to optimize your weekly selections:

  • Priority 1: Bosses that drop materials for your currently-building characters. If you are leveling Miyabi, the Dead End Butcher is mandatory.
  • Priority 2: Bosses on the highest difficulty you can reliably clear. Higher difficulty = better rewards and higher chance at 5-star Drive Discs.
  • Priority 3: Diversify your material stockpile. Even if you are not currently building a character, having a reserve of ascension materials prevents bottlenecks when you pull a new agent.
  • Efficiency Tip: Complete your three weekly boss runs as early in the week as possible (ideally Monday or Tuesday). The ascension materials may be needed for your other farming activities throughout the week.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How many weekly bosses can I claim rewards from per week?

You can claim rewards from up to 3 weekly boss fights per week in ZZZ. The weekly reset occurs on Monday at 4:00 AM server time. Each boss can be fought multiple times for practice or fun, but rewards are limited to one claim per boss per week. You can claim the same boss three times or mix and match across available bosses.

Q: What is the best team to fight the Dead End Butcher?

The Dead End Butcher is weak to Ice and Ether damage. A Miyabi or Ellen Joe mono-Ice team with Lycaon and Soukaku performs excellently, exploiting the Ice weakness for fast clears. Alternatively, Zhu Yuan with Nicole and Qingyi is highly effective thanks to Ether weakness and Nicole's grouping ability for handling Phase 2 adds.

Q: What rewards do weekly bosses drop?

Weekly bosses drop character ascension materials (boss-specific, required for leveling agents past certain thresholds), Drive Discs (including a chance at 5-star pieces), and Dennies. The ascension materials are essential — without them, your agents cannot reach their maximum potential.

Q: Can I fight weekly bosses in co-op mode?

Weekly boss fights in ZZZ are solo-only encounters. You cannot bring friends or matchmade teammates into weekly boss domains. This means your team composition and individual skill are the only factors — there is no carrying or being carried.

Q: What happens if I die during a weekly boss fight?

If your entire team is defeated, you can retry the fight from the beginning without consuming your weekly reward claim. You only consume the weekly claim when you successfully defeat the boss and choose to claim the rewards. You can retry as many times as needed to learn the fight and secure the win.

Q: Do higher difficulty levels give better rewards?

Yes. Higher difficulty levels increase the quantity of ascension materials dropped, the amount of Dennies awarded, and the probability of obtaining 5-star Drive Discs. Always fight the highest difficulty you can reliably clear. The jump from Normal to Hard difficulty approximately doubles the material yield.
