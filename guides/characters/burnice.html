📋 Character Overview

Burnice White is an S-rank Fire Anomaly character from the Sons of Calydon faction. She specializes in off-field Fire damage application through her unique Afterburn mechanic — making her one of the most valuable supports for any Disorder team composition.

Burnice's core mechanic is Fuel Spray: when she uses her EX Special Attack, she sprays a flammable fuel across a wide area. After a brief delay, the fuel ignites, creating a persistent fire zone that deals continuous Fire DMG and applies Burn anomaly buildup over 8 seconds. Crucially, this afterburn effect persists even when Burnice is swapped off the field. This allows your main DPS (like Piper or Jane Doe) to continue dealing damage while Burnice passively builds Fire anomaly in the background — creating perfect conditions for the Disorder reaction.

Strengths: Best off-field Fire application in the game — no other character can apply Fire anomaly while off-field as effectively; excellent Burn damage scaling with Anomaly Proficiency; extremely flexible team building as a secondary anomaly applier; easy to play — cast EX Special and swap off. Weaknesses: Low personal field presence — she is not designed to be a main DPS; requires another anomaly character on the team to maximize value through Disorder; energy-hungry — needs good energy management to maintain afterburn uptime; her basic attack chain is mediocre and should not be relied upon for damage.

💿 Drive Disc Recommendations

Best Set: Freedom Blues (4-Piece) + Inferno Metal (2-Piece)

Freedom Blues 4-Piece: Increases Anomaly Proficiency by 30. After triggering an anomaly (Burn), the entire team gains 20% bonus damage against that enemy for 10 seconds. This set amplifies both Burnice's personal Burn damage and your team's follow-up damage during Disorder procs.

Inferno Metal 2-Piece: Increases Fire DMG by 10%. Simple and effective — more Fire damage means stronger Burn ticks and higher Disorder damage values.

Alternative: Chaos Jazz (4-Piece) + Freedom Blues (2-Piece)

Chaos Jazz boosts Electric and Fire damage while increasing the DMG multiplier of Disorder reactions. If you're running a team where Burnice triggers frequent Disorders (e.g., Burnice + Grace), this set can outperform Freedom Blues by roughly 5-8%.

🤝 Team Compositions

Burnice is a dedicated Disorder enabler. Her teams pair her with a main anomaly DPS who applies a different element, creating massive Disorder reactions:

🥇 Best Disorder Team (Meta)

Burnice + Piper + Caesar

The most popular and strongest Disorder team in the current meta. Burnice applies off-field Fire, Piper spins up Physical Anomaly rapidly, and Caesar provides shields + ATK buff. The rotation is simple: Burnice Q/E → apply afterburn → swap to Caesar → shield → swap to Piper → spin until Physical anomaly procs → Disorder triggers for massive damage. Caesar's shield is especially valuable here because Piper's spin has no invincibility frames.

🥈 Dual Anomaly (High Investment)

Burnice + Jane Doe + Seth

Jane Doe is another anomaly character who can proc Assault (Physical anomaly). Burnice provides the off-field Fire application, creating a constant Fire/Physical Disorder cycle. Seth boosts the entire team's Anomaly Proficiency by 80, dramatically increasing both Burn and Assault damage. This team requires high investment in both anomaly characters but has the highest theoretical DPS of any Burnice team.

🥉 Electric Hybrid (F2P Accessible)

Burnice + Grace + Rina

Grace applies Electric Shock while Burnice applies Burn. When both anomalies are present, they trigger Disorder for multiplicative damage. Rina provides PEN% for the entire team, amplifying both Shock and Burn damage. This team is very F2P-friendly since Grace is an A-rank who can be obtained from the standard banner, and Rina is also a standard S-rank.

🎮 Playstyle & Rotation

Burnice is a quick-swap character. Her gameplay follows a simple rhythm:

  1. Open with Burnice: Start the fight with Burnice. Use EX Special Attack (Fuel Spray) to coat the enemy with flammable fuel. Wait 1 second for the fuel to ignite.
  2. Swap to Support/Shielder: Apply shields or buffs (Caesar's EX or Seth's shield).
  3. Swap to Main DPS: Your main anomaly DPS now attacks the burning enemy. While they build their own anomaly (Physical, Electric, etc.), Burnice's afterburn is still ticking in the background.
  4. Disorder Trigger: When your main DPS applies their anomaly, the Burn (from Burnice) + the new anomaly triggers Disorder — dealing massive burst damage equal to 150% of both anomalies' remaining damage.
  5. Refresh Afterburn: Every 8 seconds, swap back to Burnice, fire one EX Special, and immediately swap off. Never stay on Burnice longer than necessary.

💡 Pro Tip: Burnice's afterburn fuel pool persists on the ground for 10 seconds even if she hasn't ignited it yet. You can pre-cast the fuel at a choke point where the enemy will walk into it. The moment an enemy steps into the fuel pool, it ignites automatically — a great technique for speedrunning floors with predictable enemy spawn locations.

❓ FAQ

Q1: Does Burnice need her signature W-Engine?

No, but it helps significantly. Flamemaker Shaker is approximately 25% better than the next best option. However, Electro-Lip Gloss (Battle Pass) at R5 is a very solid alternative at ~85% effectiveness, and Rainforest Gourmet at R5 is ~75%. Burnice is fully functional without her signature and still enables Disorder teams effectively with any Anomaly W-Engine.

Q2: Can Burnice be a main DPS?

Technically yes, but it's not recommended. Burnice's basic attack chain and personal field time damage are significantly lower than dedicated on-field DPS characters like Ellen Joe or Zhu Yuan. Her design is clearly for off-field support — cast skill, swap off. If you try to play her as a main DPS, you'll waste her potential. She is best used as a secondary anomaly applier in a Disorder team.

Q3: How important is Energy Regen for Burnice?

Very important. Burnice needs her EX Special to apply afterburn, and each EX costs 80 Energy. With base Energy Regen, she generates roughly 1.2/s, meaning she needs about 66 seconds to generate one EX. In practice, you'll be swapping to her every 8 seconds to refresh afterburn, so she needs external Energy support or Energy Regen substats to keep up. Slot 6 Energy Regen or Swing Jazz 2-piece can dramatically improve her uptime.
