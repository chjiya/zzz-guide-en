📋 Character Overview

Ellen Joe is an S-rank Ice Attack character in Zenless Zone Zero, affiliated with Victoria Housekeeping. She wields an oversized pair of scissors as her weapon, fighting with swift, elegant slashes. Her core mechanic is the Afterimage system — landing consecutive Basic Attacks builds Afterimage stacks, and at maximum stacks (3), her next Ultimate transforms into Final Cut, a devastating Ice AOE attack with significantly enhanced damage. Ellen excels in sustained combat scenarios where she can maintain her combo chain and stack rhythm.

Ellen's damage profile differs from Miyabi's — where Miyabi goes for burst windows with Frostburn, Ellen provides consistent, high-damage field presence. Her normal attack string has 4 hits, with the 4th hit being a heavy scissor thrust that applies double Ice damage (approximately 280% ATK at level 12). Her EX Special Attack is a lunging slash that instantly grants 2 Afterimage stacks and applies 180% Ice damage, making it her primary stack-building tool. At full Afterimage stacks, Final Cut deals 350% increased Ultimate damage in a fan-shaped AOE — the main nuke in her rotation.

Strengths: Smooth, uninterrupted damage output; excellent self-buffing through the Afterimage system; strong Dodge Counter with generous invulnerability frames; her Core Passive grants 19.4% CRIT Rate, making artifact building easier. Weaknesses: Needs uninterrupted uptime to maintain Afterimage stacks — any break longer than 4 seconds resets progress; lower burst ceiling compared to Miyabi; struggles against highly mobile enemies that evade her combo chain; lacks the screen-wide AOE that Miyabi's Frostburn provides.

💿 Drive Disc Recommendations

Best Set: Polar Metal (4-Piece) + Woodpecker Electro (2-Piece)

Polar Metal 4-Piece: Increases Ice DMG by 10%. After using Ultimate, the entire team's Ice DMG increases by 20% for 12 seconds. Since Ellen uses her Ultimate every 15-18 seconds in extended fights, this buff has approximately 70% uptime. The team-wide Ice DMG buff also benefits any Ice sub-DPS or support, making this the clear best set for Ellen's team-oriented playstyle.

Woodpecker Electro 2-Piece: Increases CRIT Rate by 8%. With Ellen's Core Passive providing 19.4% CRIT Rate and this set bonus, she only needs approximately 35-40% CRIT Rate from substats to reach the optimal 65-75% range, allowing you to invest heavily in CRIT DMG.

Alternate Set: Swing Jazz (4-Piece) + Polar Metal (2-Piece)

If your team relies heavily on Chain Attacks for damage, Swing Jazz's Chain Attack damage bonus is a viable alternative. However, Polar Metal 4-piece remains superior by approximately 8-10% overall due to the team-wide Ice DMG buff.

🤝 Team Compositions

Ellen functions best as a sustained field DPS. She needs a stunner to create damage windows and a support to amplify her Ice damage.

🥇 Mono-Ice Standard (Best)

Ellen Joe + Lycaon + Soukaku

The golden standard for Ellen teams — the same core that makes Miyabi's best team also works excellently for Ellen. Lycaon shreds Ice RES by 25% and provides reliable stun application, creating 6-8 second damage windows. Soukaku provides ATK buffs (up to 1,000 ATK with full stacks) and Ice DMG% through her core passive. Triple Ice resonance gives 15% CRIT Rate. The rotation is simple: Soukaku buff → Lycaon stun → Ellen full combo (EX Special → 4-hit chain → Final Cut). This team deals approximately 45,000-50,000 DPS at moderate investment, comfortably clearing Shiyu Defense Floor 12.

🥈 Quick Stun Variant

Ellen Joe + Qingyi + Rina

This team trades Ice RES shred for faster stun application and PEN buffs. Qingyi has the highest stun application rate in the game, creating more frequent damage windows. Rina provides a team-wide PEN% buff that scales well with Ellen's high CRIT DMG investment. While the per-window damage is lower than the Mono-Ice variant (approximately 12% less per stun cycle), the increased stun frequency means you get more damage windows over the course of a fight. This team is better suited for enemies with high stun resistance where Lycaon's slower stun rate becomes a bottleneck.

🥉 Budget F2P Team

Ellen Joe + Anby + Soukaku

A reliable budget team that costs zero Polychrome. Anby is a solid A-rank stunner with quick stun application, and Soukaku is free from early game content. You lose Lycaon's Ice RES shred and Qingyi's fast stun, but the core Ice resonance is preserved. This team deals approximately 70% of the Mono-Ice variant's damage — perfectly adequate for all story content and most endgame content up to Shiyu Defense Floor 10. For F2P players, the resource saved by skipping Lycaon can be redirected to Ellen's W-Engine.

🎮 Playstyle & Rotation

Ellen's gameplay rewards consistent combo maintenance and proper stack management:

  1. Buff Phase: Start with Soukaku (E → hold attack to stack buff → Q for extended buff duration). The full Soukaku rotation takes about 3 seconds.
  2. Stun Phase: Swap to stunner (Lycaon/Anby/Qingyi) and build daze. For Lycaon, use his special combo (EX Special → hold attack → dodge counter loop) for optimal stun build-up — approximately 180 daze per rotation at level 10.
  3. Ramp Phase: When the enemy approaches stun threshold (red bar at ~80%), swap to Ellen. Use EX Special Attack to instantly gain 2 Afterimage stacks, then begin your Basic Attack combo chain to reach 3 stacks.
  4. Execute Phase: During the stun window, complete your 4-hit Basic Attack combo and immediately activate Ultimate for Final Cut. The Final Cut should be your last action in the stun window for maximum damage.
  5. Sustain Phase: If Afterimage stacks decay (after 4 seconds out of combat), use EX Special again to rebuild to 2 stacks. Your Basic Attack will bring you to 3 stacks in 2 hits.

💡 Pro Tip: Ellen's Dodge Counter does NOT reset her Basic Attack combo count. You can dodge an attack mid-combo and continue from where you left off. This is the single most important tech for maintaining Afterimage stacks against aggressive enemies. Practice this in the sparring arena against the shield Pompey mech.

⚠️ Avoid swapping off Ellen during her combo chain. Each swap resets your Afterimage stack progress and costs you 3-4 seconds of ramp-up time. Only swap if you need to dodge an unblockable attack (red flash indicator).

❓ FAQ

Q1: How does Ellen Joe's Afterimage system work exactly?

Afterimage is Ellen's stack mechanic. Each consecutive Basic Attack hit generates 1 stack (max 3). At full stacks, her Ultimate icon changes to 'Final Cut' — activating it deals 350% increased Ice damage in a cone. Each stack also provides 8% ATK (24% total at 3 stacks). Stacks decay by 1 every 4 seconds outside of combat. EX Special Attack grants 2 stacks instantly. This system rewards aggressive uptime — let your combo drop, lose your damage.

Q2: Ellen Joe vs Miyabi — who should I pull and build?

Both are S-rank Ice characters but fill different roles. Miyabi (Anomaly) has higher burst potential and faster Shiyu Defense clear times — she's better for speedrunning and AOE content. Ellen (Attack) has smoother sustained DPS, simpler rotations, and better single-target damage over long fights. If you can only build one, Miyabi has higher current meta value. However, Ellen is more forgiving for new players learning the game's combat system.

Q3: What are Ellen's ideal Drive Disc stat targets?

At endgame investment, aim for: 65-75% CRIT Rate (remember you already have 19.4% from Core Passive + 8% from Woodpecker 2-piece, so you only need ~37.6-47.6% from gear), 140-160% CRIT DMG, 2,300-2,600 ATK, and as much PEN as your substats allow. With optimal stats, her Final Cut can hit for 80,000-120,000 damage in a full rotation against level 70+ enemies with DEF shred support.

Q4: Is Lycaon required for Ellen Joe?

Lycaon is not required but highly recommended — his 25% Ice RES shred is unique and cannot be replicated by other A-rank units. Without Lycaon, use Anby + Soukaku for a budget team that works for all story content. The damage difference against elite enemies is approximately 18% in Lycaon's favor. Consider pulling Lycaon from the standard banner selector if you main Ellen.
