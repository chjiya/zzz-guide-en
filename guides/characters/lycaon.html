📋 Character Overview

Von Lycaon is an S-rank Ice Stun character in Zenless Zone Zero and the distinguished butler of Victoria Housekeeping Co. As one of the premier Stun agents in the game, Lycaon excels at rapidly filling the enemy's Daze gauge while simultaneously providing valuable Ice RES shred for Ice teams. His elegant fighting style combines swift kicks and counter-based enhancements that make him both an effective stunner and a solid source of supplementary Ice damage.

Lycaon's core gameplay revolves around his unique Dodge Counter enhancement mechanic. After executing a perfect Dodge Counter, Lycaon enters an enhanced state where his Basic Attacks apply significantly more Daze. The enhanced Basic Attack chain on his third and fourth hits deals massive Daze — roughly 80% more than his standard Basic Attack sequence. This makes him one of the most consistent stunners in the game, as his stun rotation doesn't rely on Energy or EX Special cooldowns but rather on his ability to dodge enemy attacks.

Additionally, Lycaon's Core Passive provides a team-wide Ice RES shred of 25% for 8 seconds after he hits a stunned enemy with his Special Attack or EX Special Attack. This makes him an indispensable core member of any Ice-oriented team composition. His Chain Attack and Ultimate both deal significant Ice damage with high Daze modifiers, making him valuable both as a stunner and a sub-DPS during stun windows.

Strengths: One of the fastest stun builders in the game (approximately 30% faster than A-rank stunners at equal Impact); provides 25% Ice RES shred for Ice teammates; excellent dodge counter makes him safe to play; versatile — slots into any team as a pure stunner. Weaknesses: Requires active field time for his enhanced Basic Attack stance to shine; his personal damage is modest compared to Attack characters; relies on perfect dodges to maximize stun efficiency — players who struggle with timing will see a noticeable drop in performance.

💿 Drive Disc Recommendations

Best Set: Shockstar Disco (4-Piece) + Swing Jazz (2-Piece)

Shockstar Disco 4-Piece: Increases Daze accumulation by 15%. This is the premier Stun set and Lycaon's ideal choice. The 15% Daze increase applies to all his stun sources — Basic Attacks, EX Special, Dodge Counter, Chain Attack, and Ultimate. With Shockstar Disco, his enhanced Dodge Counter → Basic Attack sequence becomes one of the fastest stun rotations available.

Swing Jazz 2-Piece: Increases Energy Regeneration by 20%. More Energy means more EX Special Attacks, which translates to more frequent Ice RES shred application and additional Daze. Since Lycaon's EX Special is one of his quickest Daze bursts (applying ~250% of his base Daze in about 0.5 seconds), having it available more often significantly improves his stun uptime.

Substat Priority

Impact% (flat) > Energy Regen > ATK% > CRIT Rate > CRIT DMG

Flat Impact substats are extremely valuable for Lycaon — every point of Impact increases all his Daze numbers. Energy Regen substats help keep EX Special uptime high. CRIT stats are nice-to-have for extra damage but should never come at the cost of Impact or ER substats.

🤝 Team Compositions

Lycaon is one of the most flexible characters in the entire game. As a Stun agent, he can fit into virtually any team that needs faster Daze accumulation. His Ice RES shred gives him an edge in Ice teams, but his raw stun speed makes him valuable everywhere.

🥇 Mono-Ice Dream Team (Best)

Lycaon + Miyabi + Soukaku

The premier Ice team and arguably the strongest composition in the current meta. Lycaon provides 25% Ice RES shred and rapid stuns, creating perfect damage windows for Miyabi's Level 3 Iai Slash and Frostburn Break. Soukaku provides massive ATK buffs (up to 1,000 ATK) and Ice DMG% from her Core Passive. The triple Ice resonance grants a bonus 15% CRIT Rate to all team members. Rotation: Soukaku buffs → Lycaon stun rotation (Dodge Counter → enhanced Basic Attack chain → EX Special) → during stun window, swap to Miyabi for her Level 3 Iai Slash → Frostburn Break → Ultimate. This team is the undisputed king of Shiyu Defense speed clears.

🥈 Universal Stunner (Most Flexible)

Any DPS + Lycaon + Any Support

Lycaon functions beautifully as a pure stunner in any team composition — he doesn't need Ice teammates to perform. The core idea is simple: Lycaon builds stun, then the DPS goes to town during the stun window. Some top pairings include: Ellen Joe + Lycaon + Soukaku (second-best Ice team, where Ellen benefits greatly from Lycaon's Ice RES shred during her burst windows); Zhu Yuan + Lycaon + Nicole (Lycaon creates consistent stun windows for Zhu Yuan's enhanced shotgun blasts); Nekomata + Lycaon + Rina (Nekomata's high multi-hit damage during stun windows synergizes well with Lycaon's fast stuns). In all these teams, Lycaon's role is the same: dodge, counter, build Daze, and tag out when the enemy is stunned.

🥉 Hypercarry Lycaon (Fun Build)

Lycaon + Rina + Soukaku

This is a meme build that turns Lycaon into the main damage dealer — not optimal for endgame, but very fun for story content and lower Shiyu floors. Build Lycaon with Ice DMG%, CRIT stats, and ATK% instead of his usual Impact-focused setup. Rina provides PEN Ratio buffs so Lycaon's Chain Attack and Ultimate hits harder, while Soukaku provides the massive ATK buff. The goal is to build stun normally, then use Lycaon's own Chain Attack and Ultimate as the nuke. His multipliers are actually quite respectable — a well-built DPS Lycaon can hit 150k+ Chain Attack crits against stunned enemies.

🎮 Playstyle & Rotation

Mastering Lycaon is about perfect dodge timing and understanding when to stay in your enhanced Basic Attack stance:

  1. Open with Support: Start the fight with your support unit (Soukaku/Rina) to apply buffs before swapping to Lycaon.
  2. Build Daze: Switch to Lycaon and look for enemy attack tells. Execute a perfect dodge to trigger Dodge Counter — this is your rotational core. The Dodge Counter itself deals good Daze, and it opens your enhanced Basic Attack stance.
  3. Enhanced Basic Attack Chain: After a successful Dodge Counter, perform the full Basic Attack sequence. The third and fourth kicks in the enhanced state apply massive Daze (~60% of his total stun output per rotation). Do NOT cancel the chain early — let the full sequence play out for maximum stun build-up.
  4. EX Special for RES Shred: Once you've built significant Daze (aim for ~60-70% of the stun bar), use EX Special Attack to apply the Ice RES shred debuff. This ensures your Ice DPS has the debuff active when the stun finally lands.
  5. Stun Window: When the enemy enters stun state, immediately swap to your main DPS. Lycaon's Chain Attack can be used if available, but be mindful not to waste the stun window timer — only use Chain Attack if your DPS hasn't already triggered it.
  6. Repeat: Swap back to Lycaon after the stun window ends to begin building the next Daze cycle.

💡 Advanced Tech: Lycaon can buffer his EX Special input during the Dodge Counter animation. Input EX Special during the Dodge Counter kick, and Lycaon will execute it immediately after the counter lands. This combo deals ~430% total Daze in about 1.5 seconds (Dodge Counter + EX Special + landing), making it the fastest single-target stun burst in the game.

⚠️ Do NOT overcommit on Lycaon. Once the enemy Daze bar is near full (80%+), swap to your DPS unit and let them trigger the stun naturally. Lycaon staying on field risks taking unnecessary damage and wastes the stun window window — your DPS should be the one benefitting from the first Chain Attack.

❓ FAQ

Q1: Is Lycaon worth pulling if I already have Qingyi?

Yes, absolutely. While both are S-rank Stun characters, they serve different roles. Qingyi provides faster general stun application and an Electric DMG% buff for Electric teams. Lycaon provides 25% Ice RES shred, making him the premier choice for Ice teams with Miyabi or Ellen Joe. They are sidegrades — if you primarily play Ice teams, Lycaon is significantly better thanks to his RES shred. If you play a variety of teams, both are valuable for different situations. Having both gives you two top-tier stunners for Shiyu Defense's two-team requirement.

Q2: Should I prioritize Lycaon's Mindscapes or his W-Engine?

Lycaon's M1 is a nice quality-of-life upgrade that increases his Dodge Counter's Daze by 20% — noticeable but not transformative. His signature W-Engine Steel Lark provides more consistent value (Energy generation + Impact%). The recommended investment path is: M0 + Steel Lark > M1 + Demara Battery Mark II > M0 + Demara Battery Mark II. In other words, grab his W-Engine first if you're willing to invest, then consider M1. M2 is a significant boost for Ice teams (extends Ice RES shred duration), but unnecessary for F2P players.

Q3: Why do I sometimes not enter the enhanced Basic Attack state after a dodge?

This usually happens when you perform an early dodge rather than a perfect dodge. Lycaon's enhanced state only activates after a perfect Dodge Counter (the one with the white flash effect). Normal dodges don't trigger it. Additionally, if you get hit during the Dodge Counter animation, the enhanced state is cancelled. Make sure you're dodging INTO the enemy attack (timing it so the attack connects during Lycaon's dodge i-frames) rather than dodging away from it — the Dodge Counter has a generous perfect dodge window of about 20 frames at 60 FPS.

Q4: What's the minimum Impact stat I should aim for?

The recommended target is 180+ Impact at level 60. With a Slot VI Impact% main stat (providing 24%), Lycaon's base 132 Impact reaches approximately 164. You'll need around 16 additional Impact from substats and/or your W-Engine to reach the 180 threshold. At 180 Impact, Lycaon stuns most bosses in about 3-4 full rotations (each rotation being Dodge Counter + enhanced Basic Attack chain). Every 10 points of Impact beyond 180 reduces your required rotations by roughly 0.3 cycles. For pure stun efficiency, every Impact substat roll is valuable — don't stop until you hit 180.
