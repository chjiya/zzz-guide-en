📋 Character Overview

Hoshimi Miyabi is an S-rank Ice Anomaly character in Zenless Zone Zero and the Section 6 (H.A.N.D.) commander. She is widely recognized as one of the strongest characters in the game, featuring a unique Frostburn mechanic — an enhanced Ice anomaly that only she can trigger. Unlike standard Freeze (which merely immobilizes enemies and deals minor shatter damage), Frostburn applies a stacking CRIT DMG amplification and culminates in a massive AOE ice explosion called Frostburn Break.

Miyabi's core gameplay revolves around building Ice anomaly accumulation through her Special Attack: Iai Slash, a three-charge technique. The first slash is a close-range vertical cut, the second extends the range horizontally, and the third is a full-screen ice explosion that instantly triggers Frostburn Break. Her charged Special Attack has three levels: Level 1 (1 stack — 180% Anomaly build-up), Level 2 (2 stacks — 360%), and Level 3 (3 stacks — 720% with guaranteed Frostburn). Each Frostburn Break deals approximately 1,200% of her ATK as Ice damage in a large area.

Strengths: Highest Ice anomaly build-up rate in the game (approximately 40% faster than any other Ice character); massive burst AOE damage through Frostburn Break; flexible team building; exceptional in both mob-clearing and boss scenarios. Weaknesses: Requires precise timing for her charged slashes — mistiming leaves her vulnerable during the 1.2-second charge animation; lower sustained damage compared to traditional Attack characters; relies heavily on her Special Attack for the majority of her damage output (about 65% of total damage comes from EX Special + Frostburn procs).

💿 Drive Disc Recommendations

Best Set: Polar Metal (4-Piece) + Freedom Blues (2-Piece)

Polar Metal 4-Piece: Increases Ice DMG by 10%. While enemies are in Frost state (Frostburn active), CRIT DMG increases by an additional 30%. This set was practically designed for Miyabi — her entire damage identity revolves around being in Frostburn state, so the conditional CRIT DMG bonus has nearly 100% uptime during her active phases.

Freedom Blues 2-Piece: Increases Anomaly Proficiency by 30. Anomaly Proficiency directly affects Frostburn build-up speed and the base damage of Frostburn Break. With Freedom Blues, Miyabi reaches approximately 148 Anomaly Proficiency from set bonuses alone, which translates to roughly 18% faster anomaly accumulation.

🤝 Team Compositions

Miyabi is remarkably flexible in team building. Her high base Frostburn efficiency means she doesn't strictly need ice resonance to perform, though it certainly helps.

🥇 Mono-Ice Dream Team (Best)

Miyabi + Lycaon + Soukaku

The undisputed best team for Miyabi. Lycaon provides Ice RES shred (25%) and the fastest stun application among Ice characters, creating perfect damage windows for Miyabi's charged slashes. Soukaku provides massive ATK buffs (up to 1,000 ATK at high investment) and Ice DMG% from her Core Passive. The triple Ice resonance boosts CRIT Rate by an additional 15%. Rotation: Soukaku E + Q to buff → Lycaon stun rotation → Miyabi swaps in during stun window → Level 3 Iai Slash → Frostburn Break → Ultimate. This team is the current speedrun meta for Shiyu Defense Floor 15.

🥈 Disorder Hybrid (High Damage)

Miyabi + Grace + Rina

This team leverages the Disorder reaction — when two different anomalies (Ice + Electric) are applied simultaneously, they trigger Disorder which deals multiplicative damage equal to 150% of both anomalies' remaining damage. Grace applies Shock quickly with her electric grenades, then Miyabi applies Frostburn to trigger Disorder. Rina provides PEN% for the entire team, amplifying both the Shock and Frostburn damage. This team has higher theoretical damage than Mono-Ice but requires precise rotation management — mistiming the anomaly application wastes the Disorder window. At high investment, this team can out-damage the Mono-Ice variant by roughly 8-10% in sustained fights.

🥉 Stun Burst (Beginner Friendly)

Miyabi + Qingyi + Soukaku

Qingyi provides the fastest stun application in the game, creating extended damage windows. Soukaku handles ATK buffing. This team is easier to play than the Disorder variant — just stun and nuke. The damage is slightly lower than Mono-Ice (about 10-12% less) because you lose the Ice RES shred from Lycaon, but the consistency makes it a great choice for players still learning Miyabi's charged slash timing.

🎮 Playstyle & Rotation

Mastering Miyabi is about timing your Iai Slash charges perfectly with stun windows:

  1. Setup Phase: Open with your support (Soukaku or Rina) to apply buffs. Swap to your stunner to build daze gauge.
  2. Pre-Charge: When the enemy's daze bar is in the red zone (about 70-80%), swap to Miyabi and begin charging her Special Attack. The goal is to have 3 stacks ready the moment the enemy enters stun state.
  3. Burst Phase: During the stun window, release the Level 3 Iai Slash. The third slash triggers Frostburn Break, dealing massive AOE Ice damage. Immediately follow up with Ultimate if available.
  4. Clean Up: Use Chain Attack procs to apply additional Ice anomaly build-up for the next Frostburn cycle. Swap back to support/stunner to begin the next rotation.
  5. Energy Management: Miyabi generates approximately 2.5 Energy per second from her passive while off-field. Use EX Special Attack (the enhanced version that costs Energy) when you need faster anomaly build-up — it applies 50% more anomaly per hit than the uncharged version.

⚠️ Critical Warning: Miyabi is NOT invincible during her Iai Slash charge animation (1.2 seconds for full charge). Do NOT charge in the middle of enemy attack patterns. Always charge during stun windows, after dodging, or during Chain Attack invulnerability frames.

💡 Advanced Tech: During Chain Attack animations, Miyabi can buffer her Special Attack input. This means she starts the Iai Slash charge during the Chain Attack invulnerability and exits the animation with a pre-charged slash. This is the speedrun tech that enables back-to-back Frostburn Breaks in consecutive stun windows.

❓ FAQ

Q1: Can Miyabi perform well at Mindscape 0? Do I need her signature W-Engine?

Yes, Miyabi at M0 is fully functional — her entire Frostburn kit is complete without Mindscapes. M1 increases Frostburn build-up by about 15% for faster rotations, and M2 is a major upgrade that makes Frostburn permanent and adds 30% CRIT DMG. However, her signature W-Engine Hailstorm Shrine provides approximately 22% total damage increase, making it the best investment. The recommended budget path is M0 + signature W-Engine rather than higher Mindscapes without it.

Q2: Why Polar Metal 4-piece and not a full ice set?

Polar Metal 4-piece gives 10% Ice DMG + 30% CRIT DMG during Frost state — this perfectly matches Miyabi's damage pattern. The full ice set's bonuses are weaker for Anomaly characters because their damage formula favors Anomaly Proficiency and CRIT DMG more than flat DMG%. The 30% CRIT DMG bonus from Polar Metal translates to roughly 18% more Frostburn Break damage, which is Miyabi's primary nuke.

Q3: What's the difference between Miyabi and Ellen Joe?

Both are S-rank Ice characters but with fundamentally different roles. Miyabi is an Ice Anomaly character specializing in burst through Frostburn procs — she builds Ice anomaly fast, triggers Frostburn for huge AOE explosions, then repeats. Ellen Joe is an Ice Attack character focused on sustained damage through Afterimage stacks — she stays on field longer, building up stacks via normal attacks and releasing Final Cut. Miyabi has higher burst and faster clear times in Shiyu Defense; Ellen has smoother sustained DPS and simpler gameplay. Current meta favors Miyabi for speed clears.

Q4: What if I don't have Lycaon or Soukaku?

Without Lycaon, use Qingyi as your stunner — she provides the fastest general stun application and creates solid damage windows. Without Soukaku, use Nicole for universal DEF shred or Lucy for ATK buffing. Even a budget team of Miyabi + Anby + Nicole clears all story content and most endgame content at reasonable investment. Miyabi's high base Anomaly Proficiency (118) and fast anomaly build-up make her self-sufficient enough to perform without ideal supports.
