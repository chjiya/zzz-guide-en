Piper Wheel Build Guide 🚛

📌 v1.0 • Updated June 4, 2026 • Physical • Anomaly • A-Rank

Overview

Piper Wheel is an A-Rank Physical Anomaly agent and a member of the Sons of Calydon faction. As a truck-driving bruiser, Piper specializes in rapidly applying the Assault anomaly effect — Physical's unique damage-over-time status that also briefly stuns enemies when triggered. Her kit revolves around her EX Special, which spins her massive wrench in a devastating AoE, accumulating Anomaly Buildup at an extraordinary rate. With sufficient Anomaly Proficiency investment, Piper can trigger Assault every 6-8 seconds, making her one of the most consistent Anomaly applicators in the game.

What makes Piper especially valuable is her role in Disorder team compositions. When Assault is applied to an enemy already affected by another Anomaly (such as Shock from Grace), the interaction triggers Disorder — a reaction that deals massive burst damage based on both Anomaly effects' remaining duration. This makes Piper one half of the most accessible and powerful F2P Disorder core in ZZZ. Additionally, as an A-Rank agent available from all banners, Piper's Cinema levels come naturally over time, with M4 and M6 providing substantial power spikes.

Piper's playstyle is straightforward but requires good timing. You want to weave her EX Special between enemy attacks since it provides super armor — she won't be interrupted during the spin. Managing her Energy is key: you need enough to fire off an EX Special whenever your cooldowns align. Her Basic Attack string is serviceable for building Anomaly between EX Specials, but your primary goal is always to have enough Energy for the next spin cycle.

Main Stat Recommendations

  • Disc 4: Anomaly Proficiency — non-negotiable. Piper's entire damage profile scales with Anomaly Proficiency. Every point increases Assault damage and Disorder damage.
  • Disc 5: Physical DMG Bonus — amplifies both Piper's direct damage and her Assault ticks. ATK% is a distant second choice.
  • Disc 6: Energy Regen or ATK% — Energy Regen lets Piper use EX Special more often, which means more Assault triggers. ATK% provides consistent stat value if you already hit Energy breakpoints.

Substat Priority

  1. Anomaly Proficiency — aim for 300+ as a baseline, 400+ ideally
  2. ATK% — scales both direct damage and Anomaly damage
  3. Energy Regen — more EX Specials equals more Assault triggers
  4. PEN — useful against high-DEF enemies, particularly in Shiyu Defense

Best Team Compositions

Rotation: Start with Grace to apply Shock → swap to Piper → use EX Special to trigger Assault and Disorder → repeat. Rina's PEN buff should be refreshed before each Disorder trigger. This rotation deals 60-80k Disorder damage per proc at high investment.

  • Lucy (Support) — Sons of Calydon synergy, ATK buff for both Physical damage dealers

Combat Rotation & Tips

  1. Opener: Use Piper's EX Special immediately to start building Anomaly Buildup. The spin hits 6-8 times and each hit applies Anomaly.
  2. Maintain Pressure: Continue with Basic Attack string (4 hits) to keep Anomaly Buildup from decaying. Anomaly gauge decays over time if not refreshed.
  3. Assault Trigger: When the Physical Anomaly circle fills, Assault triggers automatically — dealing damage and briefly stunning the enemy.
  4. Disorder Combo: If playing with Grace, ensure Shock is active BEFORE Assault triggers. The Disorder damage is based on remaining Anomaly duration, so trigger Assault as soon as Shock is applied.
  5. Energy Management: Hold EX Special when Energy is below 40 — you need enough for at least a partial spin. Piper without Energy is significantly less effective.
  6. Ultimate Usage: Save Ultimate for immediately after an Assault trigger to rapidly rebuild towards the next one. The Ultimate applies a large burst of Anomaly Buildup.

Key Tips: Piper's EX Special provides super armor — use it to tank through enemy attacks rather than dodging. In multi-enemy scenarios, her spin hits all enemies in a wide radius, making her exceptionally efficient at applying Assault to grouped mobs. Always enter a fight with at least 60 Energy to guarantee an immediate EX Special opener.

Mindscape Cinema (Duplicates) Value

  • M1: EX Special gains 15% increased Anomaly Buildup rate. ~12% more Assault triggers per fight. Highly recommended.
  • M2: Core Passive ATK stacking cap increased from 3 to 5 stacks. ~15% damage increase at full ramp. Strong power spike.
  • M4: Assault triggers restore 10 Energy. Dramatically improves Piper's rotation smoothness. ~20% QoL and damage increase.
  • M6: Assault damage increased by 30%. Every third Assault trigger deals double damage. Transformative; makes Piper competitive with S-Rank Anomaly units.

Piper has one of the best M4 breakpoints among A-Ranks. The Energy restore on Assault fundamentally changes her rotation, letting you chain EX Specials with almost no downtime. M6 makes her a legitimate endgame Anomaly carry.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is the best W-Engine for Piper?

Piper's signature W-Engine, Roaring Ride, is her best-in-slot option, providing Anomaly Proficiency and a stacking Physical DMG buff on EX Special hits. The craftable Rainforest Gourmet is an excellent F2P alternative that grants Anomaly Proficiency and Energy Regen for more frequent EX Special usage, which directly increases Assault trigger frequency.

Q: Which Drive Discs should Piper use?

The optimal setup is 4-piece Fanged Metal and 2-piece Freedom Blues. Fanged Metal grants Physical DMG Bonus and a stacking ATK buff when inflicting Assault, which Piper triggers constantly. Freedom Blues provides 20% Anomaly Proficiency which directly scales both Assault damage and Disorder reaction damage — Piper's two primary damage sources.

Q: What are Piper's best teammates?

Piper pairs excellently with Grace for Disorder teams, as the Shock + Assault combination triggers massive Disorder damage. Lucy provides invaluable team-wide ATK buffs while sharing the Sons of Calydon faction with Piper to activate both their Additional Abilities. Rina is a premium support option that extends Shock duration for easier Disorder timing.

Q: How much Anomaly Proficiency does Piper need?

Aim for at least 300 Anomaly Proficiency as a baseline, with 400+ being ideal for endgame content. Anomaly Proficiency directly scales both Assault damage and Disorder damage, making it Piper's single most important stat. Every 10 points of Anomaly Proficiency is roughly equivalent to 3-4% ATK in terms of Assault damage increase.

Q: Is Piper worth building as a new player?

Yes, absolutely. Piper is one of the best A-Rank investments in the game. She forms the core of the most accessible Disorder team (with Grace), is easy to obtain Cinema levels for, and scales well into endgame content. Her M4 and M6 breakpoints transform her from a good sub-DPS into a legitimate carry who can compete with S-Rank Anomaly agents.

Q: How does Disorder damage work with Piper?

Disorder triggers when a second Anomaly effect is applied to an enemy already affected by one. The damage is based on both Anomaly effects' remaining durations — a freshly applied Assault on a full-duration Shock deals maximum Disorder damage. This is why Piper wants to trigger Assault immediately after Grace applies Shock for optimal damage output.
