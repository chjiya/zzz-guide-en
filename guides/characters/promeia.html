📋 Character Overview

Promeia is an S-Rank Ice Anomaly agent introduced in Zenless Zone Zero version 2.8 (Phase 1). She specializes in rapid Frost accumulation — a potent Ice anomaly buildup mechanic that culminates in powerful Disorder reactions. Unlike standard anomaly agents who must commit to one element, Promeia's kit is designed to synergize with other anomaly agents, making her the premier enabler for dual-element Disorder teams.

Promeia's core gameplay revolves around applying Ice anomaly stacks at an exceptional rate. Her Special Attack and EX Special Attack apply concentrated Frost buildup across a wide area, while her Chain Attack and Ultimate deliver massive Ice damage and instantly cap the anomaly gauge on primary targets. Once Frost takes hold, she can either detonate it for substantial burst damage or allow a teammate's element to trigger Disorder, dealing multiplicative damage based on both anomalies' remaining damage potential. She has the highest base Anomaly Proficiency stat among Ice agents at 124.

Strengths: Fastest Ice anomaly buildup in the game (approximately 35% faster than standard Ice anomaly units); exceptional Disorder trigger capability with any non-Ice anomaly teammate; strong AoE presence with Frost accumulation spreading to nearby enemies; flexible in both on-field and quick-swap playstyles. Weaknesses: Lower raw personal damage compared to hypercarry anomaly agents like Jane Doe; requires a coordinated second anomaly agent to maximize her Disorder potential; cooldown management is critical to maintain consistent Frost application.

💿 Drive Disc Recommendations

Best Set: Freedom Blues (4-Piece) + Polar Metal (2-Piece)

Freedom Blues 4-Piece: Increases Anomaly Proficiency by 30 (2-piece). The 4-piece effect reduces Anomaly Buildup resistance of nearby enemies by 20% after triggering an Anomaly, further accelerating Frost application. This is Promeia's best-in-slot — it maximizes her anomaly buildup rate and supports her Disorder enabler role.

Polar Metal 2-Piece: Increases Ice DMG by 10%. A clean complement that boosts all of Promeia's Ice damage, including Frost detonation.

Alternative Set: Polar Metal (4-Piece) + Hormone Punk (2-Piece)

Polar Metal 4-Piece: Increases Ice DMG by 10% and grants an additional 30% CRIT DMG against enemies in Frost state (if applicable to Anomaly agents). For a more aggressive DPS orientation, this set amplifies Promeia's personal damage output at the cost of slower anomaly buildup.

Hormone Punk 2-Piece: Increases ATK by 10%, providing a straightforward damage multiplier.

🤝 Team Compositions

Promeia is designed as a Disorder amplifier — she applies Frost quickly, then swaps to a second anomaly agent to trigger Disorder for massive burst damage. Her best teams always include another anomaly DPS.

🥇 Ice/Fire Disorder (Best Overall)

Promeia + Burnice + Support

The premier Disorder duo. Promeia applies Frost while Burnice applies Scorched (Fire anomaly). The constant interplay between Ice and Fire triggers Disorder repeatedly, dealing multiplicative damage. Burnice's off-field afterburn maintains Fire anomaly buildup while Promeia is on-field, and vice versa. Lucy or Rina serve as the support slot for ATK buffs or PEN shred. This team has the highest sustained DPS of any Promeia composition and is the current meta standard for Shiyu Defense.

🥈 Physical/Ice Disorder

Promeia + Jane Doe + Support

Jane Doe's Assault (Physical anomaly) combined with Promeia's Frost creates devastating Disorder procs. Jane handles single-target burst through her Passion state, while Promeia provides AoE Frost buildup and crowd control. The key rotation: Jane builds Assault gauge → Promeia swaps in to layer Frost → Disorder triggers for massive damage. Soukaku or Caesar can fill the support role for ATK% buffs or shield/safety.

🥉 Electric/Ice Disorder

Promeia + Grace + Rina

Grace applies Shock quickly with her electric grenades and grenade launcher, while Promeia adds Frost to trigger Disorder. Rina provides teamwide PEN% buff, amplifying both Shock and Frost damage. This team has excellent AoE coverage and flexible rotation — either agent can drive the damage depending on enemy positioning. Slightly less single-target burst than the Burnice variant but stronger against grouped enemies.

🎮 Playstyle & Rotation

Promeia's playstyle is quick-swap Disorder enablement. Unlike on-field hypercarries, she enters, bursts Frost buildup, then tags out:

  1. Support Setup: Open with your support (Lucy/Rina/Soukaku) to apply ATK buffs or debuffs. Swap to your second anomaly agent to begin building their element's anomaly gauge.
  2. Promeia On-Field: Swap to Promeia. Use EX Special Attack to apply heavy Frost buildup in a wide AoE. The EX Special applies approximately 240% Anomaly Buildup at base, scaling with AP. Follow up with basic attacks to maintain Frost pressure.
  3. Disorder Trigger: Once Frost is applied (enemy has Ice anomaly), immediately swap to your second anomaly agent (Burnice/Jane/Grace) and use their EX Special to apply their element. The overlapping anomalies trigger Disorder, dealing massive damage proportional to both anomaly ticks remaining.
  4. Repeat: Swap back to Promeia once energy is available for another EX Special. Continue the Frost → Element → Disorder loop. Use Promeia's Ultimate as a panic button or during stun windows — it instantly maxes Frost buildup and deals high burst damage.
  5. Energy Management: Promeia generates approximately 2.8 Energy/s from her passive while off-field. With Energy Regen on Slot VI and her signature W-Engine, she can maintain approximately 70% uptime on EX Special spam, which is sufficient for consistent Disorder rotations.

💡 Pro Tech: Promeia's EX Special has a brief startup animation that is fully invulnerable. Use this to dodge through enemy attacks while simultaneously applying Frost. This is similar to Burnice's EX Special dodge cancel tech and is essential for aggressive play at high difficulty levels.

⚠️ Do NOT stay on Promeia longer than necessary. Her personal damage is lower than dedicated on-field anomaly DPS. The goal is to apply Frost, swap out, and trigger Disorder. Overstaying on Promeia reduces team DPS by delaying Disorder cycles.

❓ FAQ

Q1: Is Promeia worth pulling at M0?

Yes. Promeia is fully functional at M0 — her entire Disorder enabler kit is present without any Mindscapes. M1 increases her Energy generation by 20%, smoothing out rotations. M2 enhances Frost buildup by 25% and adds a teamwide Anomaly Proficiency buff. However, her signature W-Engine (Frostveil Requiem) provides a larger damage gain (~25%) than M1 (~12%). Recommended budget path: M0 + signature W-Engine.

Q2: Freedom Blues or Polar Metal — which is better?

Freedom Blues 4-Piece is generally better for Promeia because her primary role is Disorder enabler, not personal DPS. The 20% Anomaly Buildup resistance reduction on the 4-piece set helps both Promeia and her teammate apply anomalies faster, resulting in more frequent Disorder ticks. Polar Metal 4-Piece is viable if you want her to contribute more personal damage, but overall team DPS is higher with Freedom Blues in coordinated teams.

Q3: Can Promeia work in a mono-Ice team?

Technically yes, but it's suboptimal. Promeia's kit is designed around hybrid-element Disorder teams. In mono-Ice, she loses access to Disorder reactions entirely, which removes approximately 40% of her potential damage contribution. If you must run mono-Ice, pair her with Soukaku for ATK buffs and Lycaon for Ice RES shred — but you will see significantly lower clear times than any Disorder variant.

Q4: What is the minimum Anomaly Proficiency target for Promeia?

Aim for at least 320 total Anomaly Proficiency from base stats, Drive Disc main stats/substats, set bonuses, and W-Engine combined. Beyond 360 AP, the returns are still good but begin to diminish slightly. The ideal endgame target is 380-420 AP with good ATK% substats. Every 10 points of AP translates to approximately 2.5% higher Frost buildup speed and 1.8% higher Disorder damage.
