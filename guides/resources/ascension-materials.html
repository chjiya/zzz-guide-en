📋 Overview

Ascension materials are the core resources you need to level up your agents in Zenless Zone Zero. Every character requires a combination of Element Chips, Enemy Drop Materials, and Weekly Boss Drops to ascend past level 20, 40, 50, and 60 breakpoints. Skill upgrades also consume vast quantities of these materials, making efficient farming essential for endgame progression.

This guide provides a complete reference for every material type, every character's material requirements organized by faction, and the most stamina-efficient farming strategies. Whether you are pre-farming for an upcoming banner or trying to max your current carries, this is your one-stop resource for all ascension-related questions.

💡 Key Insight: Pre-farming materials before a character's banner releases can save you up to two weeks of dedicated farming. Always check what materials a new character needs and start farming at least 2-3 weeks before their release!

⚡ Stamina Budget: A single S-rank character needs approximately 8,000-9,000 total stamina to reach max level with all skills at level 12. Plan your resources accordingly and focus on one team at a time.

🗓️ Weekly Boss Materials

Weekly boss materials are used for Core Skill upgrades (the outermost skill enhancement ring on a character's skill screen). These are the scarcest materials in the game because Notorious Hunts are limited to 3 attempts per week at 60 stamina each. Each character needs 9 total of their corresponding weekly boss material to fully max their Core Skill to level 12.

⏰ Weekly Boss Strategy: Always use all 3 Notorious Hunt attempts before Sunday weekly reset. If you are building a specific character, focus their boss exclusively — the 3-attempt-per-week cap means you need 3 full weeks to get the 9 materials needed for max Core Skill on a single S-rank character.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Q1: How long does it take to fully max one S-rank character?

Approximately 29-37 days of dedicated stamina usage (240 stamina/day). This includes character ascension, skill materials, weekly boss farming (3 weeks minimum for Core Skill), W-Engine leveling, and Dennies. Focus on getting your main team functional (level 55, skills level 8-10) before maxing any single character.

Q2: Should I use batteries during double-drop events?

Absolutely yes. Save all your battery reserves for double-drop events (usually one per patch). Using 30 batteries during a double-drop week effectively doubles your stamina output for that week. This is roughly 2x more efficient than using batteries outside of events. If you urgently need materials for a Shiyu Defense clear, using 1-2 batteries is fine, but avoid burning your entire reserve.

Q3: What's the best way to farm enemy drop materials?

For free (no stamina): run open-world enemy routes daily. Each route takes 8-15 minutes and gives you enough Basic/Advanced materials for regular ascensions. For stamina efficiency: run the highest Combat Simulation level you can clear. The level 60+ domain guarantees Advanced and Specialized materials. Never run lower-level domains — the material-to-stamina ratio is significantly worse.

Q4: Can I convert lower-tier materials into higher-tier ones?

Yes. ZZZ has a material synthesis system in the item menu. You can combine 3 Basic chips to make 1 Advanced chip, and 3 Advanced chips to make 1 Specialized chip. This uses no stamina but does consume Dennies. It's generally not recommended to synthesize lower-tier materials unless you're in a rush — it's more efficient to farm the correct tier directly. However, if you have hundreds of Basic chips from open-world farming, synthesis is a perfectly valid way to get value from them.

Q5: What materials should I pre-farm for an unreleased character?

If you know a character's element (from leaks), start farming the corresponding Element Chips and Weekly Boss materials. If you know their faction, you can guess their enemy drop material. Generic items (Dennies, EXP materials, skill materials) are always safe to pre-farm. As a general rule: start pre-farming 2-3 weeks before a banner you want to pull on.

Q6: How do I get more Chip materials quickly?

The fastest method is Combat Simulation at the highest difficulty you can clear. The level 60+ domains give the best drop rates per stamina. You can also purchase chip material boxes from the Hollow Zero shop (using Investigation Points) and the Signal Shop (using Residual Signal currency). These are great filler purchases if you have extra currency.
