💰 Polychrome Overview

Polychromes are the premium currency in Zenless Zone Zero. They are used to pull on banners (160 Polychromes = 1 pull), refresh stamina (50 Polychromes per 60 stamina on the first 3 daily refreshes), and purchase limited-time shop items.

As a F2P player, you can earn approximately 8,000-12,000 Polychromes per patch (6-week cycle), which translates to 50-75 pulls. With the monthly pass (Interknot Subscription), this increases to approximately 11,000-15,000 Polychromes per patch (70-95 pulls including the subscription currency).

This guide breaks down every single Polychrome source in the game, including one-time rewards, recurring income, and event earnings. Use this to plan your savings for upcoming banners.

Daily Commissions (60 Polychromes/day): Complete all 3 daily tasks in the Inter-Knot app. This takes 5-10 minutes. The tasks are usually: consume 60 stamina (do one run of any material domain), complete 1 story/hollow zero commission, and collect from your video store revenue. Always prioritize this — missing a single day costs you 60 Polychromes (0.375 pulls).

Hollow Zero (40-80 Polychromes/week): Earned by accumulating Investigation Points in Hollow Zero. You can earn 4,000 points per week for the full 80 Polychromes. Completing one full Hollow Zero clear (any difficulty) usually gives enough points for the week. This also gives valuable Bangboo EXP and materials.

Bounty Commissions (60 Polychromes/week): Complete 3 Bounty Commissions from the Scott Outpost. These are weekly missions like "Complete 3 Notorious Hunt fights" or "Clear Hollow Zero once." The reward package also includes Dennies and W-Engine materials.

⚔️ Shiyu Defense Rewards

Shiyu Defense is the primary endgame content and the largest source of recurring Polychromes. It resets every 2 weeks and has two categories:

Stable Node (Floors 1-7): A fixed challenge that rewards 300 Polychromes per clear. Total rewards: 300 Polychromes per 2 weeks (600 per month). The difficulty is moderate — a team of level 50 characters with level 40 W-Engines can clear this.

Critical Node (Floors 5-7 only): The rotating endgame challenge. Rewards depend on your clear rating (S, A, B, C). The full S-rank clear gives 320-480 Polychromes per rotation depending on the seasonal difficulty modifier — higher difficulty seasons offer more rewards to compensate for the increased challenge.

⚡ Farming Efficiency Tips

  1. Never spend Polychromes on standard banner pulls. Blue Master Tapes are given for free through Battle Pass, character ascension, and events. Every Polychrome spent on the standard banner is a pull you're not saving for a limited character.
  2. Empty your stamina daily. Daily commission rewards require 60 stamina consumption. Even if you don't need materials, run the highest-level resource domain and stockpile. Wasted stamina is wasted Polychrome value (60 stamina is worth 50 Polychromes in refresh cost).
  3. Maximize Hollow Zero weekly. The weekly 80 Polychromes from Hollow Zero Investigation Points is free. One full clear (30-40 minutes) gives enough points if you open chests and complete side objectives.
  4. Don't refresh stamina more than 3 times per day. The cost escalates to 100/150/200 after the first 3 refreshes. Only whales should refresh more than 3 times. F2P players should do 0-1 refreshes per day max.
  5. Complete every event stage. Even if the event seems boring or difficult, most ZZZ events have generous participation rewards. The first 3-4 stages of any event typically give 60-80% of the total Polychrome rewards.
  6. Claim Signal Shop items monthly. The Signal Shop (exchanging residual signals from duplicate pulls) gives 5 Master Tapes and 5 Encrypted Master Tapes per month. These are effectively free pulls — don't forget to claim them.

❓ FAQ

Q1: How many Polychromes can I save before Caesar's banner ends?

If you start from 0 and play actively for the remaining 14 days of Caesar's banner, you can earn approximately 3,000-4,000 Polychromes from dailies (60×14=840), Shiyu Defense (if it resets during that period, 600-800), events (500-1,000), and one-time sources (1,000-2,000 for new accounts). This gives you roughly 19-25 pulls. Combined with available Master Tapes, you could realistically reach 30-40 pulls total. If you already have some savings or are a new player with untapped one-time sources, you might reach pity.

Q2: Is the Interknot Monthly Pass worth it?

Yes, the $5 Interknot Monthly Pass is the best value purchase in ZZZ. It gives 3,000 Polychromes over 30 days (100 per day) — that's about 18.75 pulls for $5, or roughly $0.27 per pull. Compare this to direct Polychrome purchases where $100 gives approximately 50 pulls ($2.00 per pull). The monthly pass is approximately 7.4x more efficient than direct top-up. If you spend any money on ZZZ, start with the monthly pass and only consider other purchases if you still need more pulls.

Q3: Should I skip a banner to save for a future one?

Yes, if the current banner character doesn't fit your account. ZZZ's Power creep is minimal, so skipping a banner to guarantee a future must-pull like Caesar is completely reasonable. The F2P income of ~36 pulls per month means you can only guarantee one S-rank every 2-3 months. Be selective. Skip characters who: (a) need specific team members you don't have, (b) overlap in role with characters you already have built, or (c) are from an element/class that doesn't fit your current account direction.

Q4: How do maintenance compensation and bug fix Polychromes work?

Every patch maintenance gives 300 Polychromes (approximately 2 pulls) as compensation. Additional bug fix compensations (60-300 Polychromes) are occasionally sent when major issues are fixed. These are automatically added to your account — you do not need to claim them. Over a year, maintenance compensations alone give approximately 2,400-3,000 Polychromes (15-19 pulls).
