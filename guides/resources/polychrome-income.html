Polychrome Basics — The Universal Premium Currency

Polychrome is Zenless Zone Zero's premium currency, used primarily for pulling characters, W-Engines, and Bangboo on gacha banners. Understanding how many Polychromes you can earn per month is the single most important skill for F2P and light spender account management — it determines how many limited characters you can guarantee, which banners you can afford to skip, and how aggressively you can vertically invest in your favorite agents.

Pull Conversion: 160 Polychrome = 1 pull (Master Tape or Encrypted Tape). This means every 1,600 Polychrome = 10 pulls. All calculations below use this conversion rate. A hard pity on the character event banner is 90 pulls (14,400 Polychrome), and soft pity typically triggers around pull 75 (12,000 Polychrome). The W-Engine banner has 80-pull hard pity (12,800 Polychrome) with soft pity at ~65 pulls (10,400 Polychrome).

📊 Key Numbers to Remember: 1 pull = 160 Polychrome. 10 pulls = 1,600 Polychrome. 1 month of F2P income ≈ 75-85 pulls (12,000-13,600 Polychrome). 1 patch (6 weeks) of F2P income ≈ 110-125 pulls (17,600-20,000 Polychrome). 1 guaranteed limited S-rank (losing 50/50) ≈ 180 pulls (28,800 Polychrome).

Shiyu Defense & Hollow Zero Detailed Payout

These two endgame modes provide your most consistent permanent Polychrome income. Here is the precise breakdown per cycle:

Shiyu Defense (Critical Node): Resets every 2 weeks. Maximum payout requires clearing all 15 floors with S-rank (timed clear). Each floor from 6-15 gives 80 Polychrome (floors 1-5 give materials only). Total per reset: 800 Polychrome. Monthly (2 resets): 1,600 Polychrome from the front node. However, there is also a back node that resets simultaneously with ~400 Polychrome per clear. Total Shiyu Defense monthly: ~2,400 Polychrome.

Hollow Zero / Lost Void Weekly: 3 weekly bounties at Hard+ difficulty give 80 Polychrome each = 240 per week. Monthly (4 weeks): 960 Polychrome. Additionally, first-clear bonuses for each difficulty tier give one-time Polychrome (500 for Normal, 800 for Hard, 1,200 for Extreme, 1,500 for Nightmare) — these are one-time, do not budget them.

How Many Banners You Can Guarantee Per Patch

This is the question every player ultimately wants answered. The banner system works as follows:

  • Character Event Banner: Hard pity at 90 pulls. Soft pity starts at ~75 pulls. 50/50 chance to get the featured character. If you lose the 50/50, your next S-rank is guaranteed to be the featured character.
  • W-Engine Event Banner: Hard pity at 80 pulls. Soft pity at ~65 pulls. 75/25 chance to get the featured W-Engine (significantly better odds than character banner).
  • Permanent Banner: Hard pity at 90 pulls. No limited characters. Do not spend Polychrome here — use only free standard banner tapes.

Key Takeaways for F2P and Light Spenders

  • Never pull on the standard banner with Polychrome. Free standard tapes only. Every Polychrome spent on standard banner is 160 Polychrome that could have been a limited banner pull.
  • Skip at least 40-50% of banners as F2P. You cannot get every character. Prioritize characters that fill a role your account lacks or that you genuinely enjoy.
  • The Inter-Knot Membership is 5x better than direct top-up. If you plan to spend any money, start here. It converts $5 into ~17 pulls, while $5 of direct top-up gives ~3 pulls.
  • Plan 2 patches ahead. Leaks and official announcements typically give 6-8 weeks of advance notice for upcoming characters. Use this window to adjust your savings plan.
  • Don't impulse-pull on new character banners. Wait 3-5 days after a banner launches for community reviews and gameplay impressions. Some characters look powerful on paper but underperform in practice (or require specific teammates you may not have).
  • Your most valuable resource is patience. A F2P player who saves carefully for 3 patches can guarantee any single character with their signature W-Engine. Skipping 2-3 banners is a small price for getting your dream character fully equipped.
