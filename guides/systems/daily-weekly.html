Daily & Weekly Checklist 📋

📌 v1.0 • Updated June 4, 2026 • Routine • Efficiency • Time Management

Overview

Consistency is the key to account progression in Zenless Zone Zero. While the game is generous with one-time rewards from story and exploration, the bulk of your ongoing Polychrome income, upgrade materials, and progression resources come from repeatable daily and weekly activities. Missing these adds up quickly — skipping a week of Notorious Hunt or Hollow Zero can set your Talent upgrades back significantly.

This checklist consolidates every daily and weekly activity in ZZZ into a streamlined routine that maximizes rewards while minimizing time spent. Whether you're a busy professional with 5 minutes to spare or a dedicated player with an hour to optimize, there's a cadence here that fits your schedule.

⏰ Daily Checklist (~15-20 min)

Complete these every day, ideally in one session. The order is optimized for efficiency:

1. Coffee at Coff Cafe (1 min)

  • Teleport to Sixth Street and visit the Coff Cafe (near the Video Store).
  • Drink the daily coffee — it costs Dennies and grants +60 Battery Charge.
  • Different coffee types exist (some give bonus effects like extra material drops), but the Battery Charge is the important part. Always get at least the basic coffee.
  • Reward: +60 Battery Charge (worth 60 Polychrome-equivalent in stamina value).

2. Scratch Card (1 min)

  • Visit the newsstand (Howl's Newsstand) on Sixth Street, near the metro entrance.
  • Scratch your daily Scratch Card — it's a simple scratch-off lottery that awards Dennies, upgrade materials, or Polychromes.
  • There's no strategy to this — the reward is predetermined. Just scratch and collect.
  • Reward: Variable (Dennies, materials, or Polychromes). Small but adds up over a patch cycle.

3. Video Store Management (1 min)

  • Enter the Video Store (Random Play) on Sixth Street.
  • Check the counter for daily rental income. The Video Store generates passive Dennies based on your store level.
  • Occasionally, customers will appear with special requests that grant bonus rewards.
  • Reward: Passive Dennies income. Upgrading the store via Inter-Knot progression increases the daily yield.

4. Daily Errands (5 min)

  • Open the Errands menu (Inter-Knot → Errands) and claim or complete your 4 daily missions.
  • Missions typically involve: defeating a certain number of enemies, completing a specific activity type, using specific agents, or spending Battery Charge.
  • Complete these efficiently by running activities that also progress your daily stamina spending.
  • Reward: 60 Polychromes + Inter-Knot EXP + upgrade materials. This is your primary daily Polychrome source.

5. Spend All Battery Charge (10-15 min)

  • After coffee, you should have around 180-300 Battery Charge (depending on when you last logged in).
  • Spend it efficiently based on your account stage (see the Stamina Guide for detailed priorities).
  • Quick run-down of options:
    • Before IK 40: Combat Simulation (Agent EXP) → Talent Materials → W-Engine Materials.
    • After IK 40: Routine Cleanup (Drive Discs) — this will be your daily stamina sink for most of the endgame.
  • Reward: Agent/W-Engine EXP, Talent materials, Drive Discs — the core progression loop.

6. Battle Pass Daily Missions (passive)

  • Most Battle Pass daily missions complete automatically as you do the above tasks (spend stamina, complete Errands, defeat enemies).
  • Check the Battle Pass screen after your daily routine and claim any completed missions.
  • Reward: Battle Pass EXP progressing toward the next level. Free track gives materials; paid track gives additional Encrypted Master Tapes and resources.

📅 Weekly Checklist (~1-2 hours across the week)

These activities reset on Monday at 04:00 (server time). Spread them throughout the week or knock them out in one session:

1. Notorious Hunt — 3 Free Attempts

  • Priority: 🔴 HIGH — do NOT miss these. Weekly boss materials are time-gated and cannot be farmed elsewhere.
  • Complete all 3 free attempts at the highest difficulty you can clear comfortably.
  • Cost: 60 Battery each (180 total = 3 daily stamina cycles).
  • Target the boss that drops materials for your current priority agents. See the Notorious Hunt Guide for boss-specific strategies.
  • Reward: Rare Talent upgrade materials, Dennies, Agent EXP, chance at Drive Discs.

2. Hollow Zero — Cap Investigation Merits

  • Priority: 🔴 HIGH — primary source of Agent Talent and W-Engine upgrade materials from the Outpost Logistics shop.
  • Run the highest difficulty you can handle (Withering Garden is most efficient).
  • Typically takes 2-3 Core runs or 1-2 Withering Garden runs to cap weekly merits.
  • Cost: 0 Battery Charge. Hollow Zero is stamina-free — a major advantage.
  • Spend your Investigation Merits at the Outpost Logistics shop (at Scott Outpost) before the weekly reset.
  • Reward: Investigation Merits (exchange for talent/W-Engine materials), Resonium, Agent EXP, Dennies.

3. Shiyu Defense (Bi-Weekly)

  • Priority: 🟡 MEDIUM-HIGH — one of the best recurring Polychrome sources.
  • Clears should be attempted after you've spent weekly stamina on upgrades for your teams.
  • Aim for at least Floor 8 clear (240 Polychromes). Full S-Rank clear of all Critical Node floors yields 720 Polychromes per cycle.
  • Resets every 2 weeks (Wednesday or Thursday, depending on server).
  • Reward: Polychromes, Dennies, upgrade materials, Investigation Merits.

4. Battle Pass Weekly Missions

  • Priority: 🟢 MEDIUM — valuable but most complete passively.
  • Weekly missions include: spend X Battery Charge, defeat X enemies, complete X Notorious Hunt runs, complete X Hollow Zero runs.
  • If you're doing the above weekly activities, these should complete automatically.
  • Check before Monday reset and mop up any incomplete missions if you're near a BP level threshold.
  • Reward: Battle Pass EXP, progressing toward free and paid track rewards.

5. Signal Shop — Monthly Reset

  • At the start of each month, the Signal Shop restocks 5 discounted Encrypted Master Tapes and 5 discounted Master Tapes.
  • Buy these with Fading Signals (the currency from B-Rank duplicates) — they're the best value in the shop.
  • Reward: 5 Encrypted Tapes + 5 Standard Tapes per month at a discount.

🚀 Speed-Run Routine (Under 5 Minutes)

On busy days when you can only spare 5 minutes, here's the bare minimum:

  1. Teleport to Sixth Street: Drink coffee → Scratch Card → Collect Video Store income. (2 min)
  2. Open Errands menu: Claim all completed Errands. Accept new ones. (30 sec)
  3. Spend Battery Charge: Run Combat Simulation or W-Engine material domain on auto-clear (if available) or quick-clear the cheapest option. The goal is to empty your stamina, not optimize — 5 runs of any domain beats wasting stamina. (2-3 min)
  4. Claim Battle Pass: Quick check and claim. (15 sec)

On these days, you sacrifice optimization for speed. Your weekly Notorious Hunt and Hollow Zero can wait for a day when you have more time.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What should I do daily in Zenless Zone Zero?

Your daily routine should include (in order): drinking coffee at the Coff Cafe (+60 Battery Charge), scratching your daily Scratch Card at Howl's Newsstand, collecting Video Store income, completing your 4 daily Errand missions (60 Polychromes), spending all your Battery Charge on progression activities, and claiming Battle Pass daily missions. This takes approximately 15-20 minutes total.

Q: How long does the daily routine take in ZZZ?

A complete daily routine takes approximately 15-20 minutes: 5 minutes for Errands, coffee, Scratch Card, and Video Store management, and 10-15 minutes for spending Battery Charge (typically 5 Routine Cleanup runs at 60 each). On busy days, the routine can be compressed to under 5 minutes by spending stamina on fast-clear domains like Agent EXP or W-Engine material stages.

Q: What weekly activities should I prioritize?

Weekly priorities in order of importance: (1) Notorious Hunt — all 3 free attempts, these are time-gated Talent materials you can't farm elsewhere. (2) Hollow Zero — cap your weekly Investigation Merits for the Outpost Logistics shop. (3) Shiyu Defense — full clear every cycle for Polychromes. (4) Battle Pass weekly missions — these mostly complete passively from the above activities. All weekly activities reset on Monday at 04:00 server time except Shiyu Defense which is bi-weekly.

Q: What happens if I miss a day of dailies?

Missing a day means you lose 60 Polychromes (from daily Errands), 240+ Battery Charge (from natural regeneration + coffee), and a Scratch Card. It's a setback but not account-breaking. The game is designed so missing occasional days doesn't cripple your progression. Just don't make it a habit — consistent daily play adds up to roughly 1,800 Polychromes per month from Errands alone.

Q: Should I do Hollow Zero or Shiyu Defense first each week?

Do Notorious Hunt first on Monday (it uses the most Battery Charge and gates your Talent upgrades). Then do Hollow Zero (it costs no stamina, so you can run it between stamina refreshes). Shiyu Defense should be done after you've used your weekly stamina to upgrade your agents — attempting it on Monday before spending stamina is fine, but you might have an easier time later in the week with freshly upgraded gear.
