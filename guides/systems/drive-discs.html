Drive Disc System Guide 💿

📌 v1.0 • Updated June 4, 2026 • Gear System • Stats • Farming

Overview

Drive Discs are Zenless Zone Zero's equipment system — the primary way you customize and empower your agents beyond their base stats and Talent levels. Each agent can equip up to 6 Drive Discs in numbered slots (1 through 6), and each disc provides a combination of a fixed main stat and up to 4 random substats. Equipping multiple discs from the same set (2-piece and 4-piece bonuses) grants powerful synergistic effects that can dramatically alter an agent's performance.

Drive Discs are the single biggest source of agent power in the endgame. A well-built agent with optimized Drive Discs can deal 2-3× more damage than the same agent with random, unleveled discs. Because substats are randomized, farming perfect Drive Discs is a long-term goal that keeps even veteran players engaged with the Routine Cleanup system at the HIA Club.

Understanding the Drive Disc system — which sets are best, which main stats to prioritize on each slot, how to efficiently farm and tune discs, and when to stop farming — is essential knowledge for every ZZZ player aiming to clear Shiyu Defense and high-difficulty Hollow Zero content.

Drive Disc Basics

Slot System

Drive Discs occupy 6 numbered slots. Each slot has a fixed main stat pool:

Rarity Tiers

Drive Discs come in three rarities, indicated by color:

  • B-Rank (Blue): Max level 9. 2 substats. Discard immediately after early game.
  • A-Rank (Purple): Max level 12. 3 substats. Serviceable for mid-game and as placeholder pieces.
  • S-Rank (Gold): Max level 15. 4 substats. The only tier worth investing resources into long-term. Guaranteed drops at IK 40+.

Set Bonuses

Each Drive Disc belongs to a set. Equipping 2 pieces or 4 pieces from the same set activates its bonus effects. The standard configuration is 4-piece (main set) + 2-piece (off-set) to maximize the 4-piece bonus while gaining a valuable 2-piece bonus from a complementary set.

Substat Priority & Optimization

Substats make or break a Drive Disc. A "god-tier" disc is one where the substats align perfectly with the agent's needs. Here's the universal substat priority:

  1. CRIT Rate / CRIT DMG — Double-crit substats on the same piece are the holy grail. Prioritize pieces that roll both.
  2. ATK% — Universally valuable for all DPS agents. Aim for ATK% on every piece.
  3. Anomaly Proficiency — Critical for Anomaly agents; a dead stat for traditional DPS.
  4. Flat ATK — Small additive gain. Better than DEF or HP.
  5. PEN — Niche stat for high-DEF enemy content. Low priority.

Baseline targets for endgame DPS: 60%+ CRIT Rate, 120%+ CRIT DMG, 2500+ ATK with your Drive Discs fully leveled.

Leveling & Resource Management

Drive Discs are leveled using Drive Disc EXP materials and Dennies. The cost escalates significantly at higher levels:

  • Levels 1-9: Cheap — safe to level promising pieces to see their substat rolls.
  • Levels 10-12: Moderate cost. Only level pieces that have at least 2 good substats.
  • Levels 13-15: Expensive. Reserve for pieces that have rolled well at levels 1-12. A single level 15 disc costs substantial resources.

Dismantling: Unwanted leveled Drive Discs can be dismantled at the Bardic Needle for Plating Agent materials. You recover approximately 80% of the EXP invested, making it worthwhile to level promising but ultimately disappointing discs.

Bardic Needle (Tuning)

The Bardic Needle is a specialized system (unlocked via the HIA Club) that allows targeted Drive Disc crafting. Instead of farming random drops, you use Plating Agents (obtained from dismantling discs) and Tuning Calibrators to craft discs for specific slots and sets:

  • Target Slot: Choose which slot (1-6) you want. Most valuable for slots 4, 5, and 6 where main stats vary.
  • Target Set: Specify which Drive Disc set to pull from.
  • Main Stat Lock (Tuning Calibrator): Rare item that guarantees a specific main stat on the crafted disc. Save these exclusively for Disc 5 (Elemental DMG Bonus) and Disc 4 (CRIT stats).

Bardic Needle is your best tool for completing builds when you're one specific piece away. Never use Tuning Calibrators on slots 1-3 or on non-critical main stats.

Farming Strategy

When to Farm

  • Inter-Knot 20-35: Do not farm. Use what drops from story and events. Save your Battery Charge.
  • Inter-Knot 35-40: Start light farming in Routine Cleanup. A-Rank discs with correct main stats are enough.
  • Inter-Knot 40+: S-Rank discs are guaranteed per run. This is the optimal time to seriously farm. Spend the majority of your daily Battery Charge here until your main DPS agents are decently geared.
  • Inter-Knot 45+: Endgame farming. Aim for double-crit substats and push for S-Rank Shiyu Defense clears.

Efficiency Tips

  1. Farm domains that drop sets for multiple agents you're building. For example, the domain that drops both Woodpecker Electro and Shockstar Disco serves both DPS and Stun agents.
  2. Keep any S-Rank Disc 4 with CRIT Rate or CRIT DMG, Disc 5 with Elemental DMG Bonus, and Disc 6 with ATK% or Energy Regen — even if the set isn't ideal.
  3. Don't obsess over perfect substats for Support and Defense agents. Correct main stats at level 12 are sufficient for these roles.
  4. Use the Bardic Needle to fill gaps after spending time farming domains, not as a primary acquisition method.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What are Drive Discs in Zenless Zone Zero?

Drive Discs are Zenless Zone Zero's equipment system — equippable gear items that provide stat bonuses to agents. Each agent can equip 6 Drive Discs (numbered 1-6), and equipping multiple discs from the same set grants powerful set bonuses at 2-piece and 4-piece thresholds. They are the primary source of agent customization and power progression.

Q: What is the best Drive Disc set for DPS agents?

The optimal Drive Disc setup varies by agent element and playstyle. Generally, elemental sets (Polar Metal for Ice, Inferno Metal for Fire, Thunder Metal for Electric, Chaotic Metal for Ether, Fanged Metal for Physical) as 4-piece combined with 2-piece Woodpecker Electro for CRIT Rate is the universal DPS template. Some agents prefer 4-piece Woodpecker Electro if they rely heavily on Basic Attacks.

Q: How do I farm Drive Discs efficiently?

Farm Drive Discs at the HIA Club's Routine Cleanup using Battery Charge. Focus on domains that drop sets for multiple agents you're building. At Inter-Knot Level 40+, gold (S-Rank) discs become guaranteed drops per run. Save Bardic Needle tuning materials and Tuning Calibrators to target specific slots and main stats for your most important agents.

Q: Should I use ATK% or Elemental DMG on Disc 5?

Always use Elemental DMG Bonus on Disc 5 for DPS agents. The damage formula treats Elemental DMG as a separate multiplier from ATK%, so Elemental DMG Bonus provides substantially more total damage than an equivalent amount of ATK%. The only exception is if your agent uses multiple elements or scales off a non-elemental stat like Anomaly Proficiency.

Q: When should I stop farming Drive Discs for an agent?

A reasonable stopping point: correct main stats on all 6 slots at S-Rank level 15, with at least 2 relevant substats per piece. For your main DPS, aim for double-crit (CRIT Rate + CRIT DMG) on Discs 1-3 and at least one crit stat on 4-6. Perfection-seeking beyond this point yields diminishing returns and is only recommended for your absolute favorite agents.
