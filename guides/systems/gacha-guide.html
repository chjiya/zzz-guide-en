Gacha System Guide 🎰

📌 v1.0 • Updated June 4, 2026 • Banners • Pity • Polychromes • Strategy

Overview

The gacha system is the core acquisition mechanic in Zenless Zone Zero — the way you obtain new agents, W-Engines, and Bangboo through a randomized pull system using premium and free currencies. Understanding how the banner types, pity mechanics, 50/50 system, and soft pity thresholds work is essential for managing your resources efficiently, especially as a F2P or low-spending player.

ZZZ uses a multi-banner system similar to Genshin Impact and Honkai: Star Rail. There are four main banner types: the Exclusive Channel (limited character banner), the W-Engine Channel (limited weapon banner), the Stable Channel (standard/permanent banner), and the Bangboo Channel. Each uses different currencies and operates on slightly different pity mechanics.

Smart pulling strategy — knowing when to save, when to pull, and how to evaluate a banner's value — can double your effective account power over time compared to pulling impulsively. This guide breaks down every aspect of ZZZ's gacha economy.

Pity System Deep Dive

Soft Pity

The most important concept for efficient pulling. While hard pity is listed at 90 (character) and 80 (W-Engine), the reality is that soft pity kicks in much earlier. Starting at pull 75 on the character banner (65 on W-Engine), the S-Rank probability increases with each pull, creating a bell curve of outcomes:

  • Pulls 1-74: Flat 0.6% per pull. Most players will NOT get an early S-Rank.
  • Pull 75: Rate begins climbing steeply. ~20% cumulative chance of S-Rank.
  • Pull 76-78: Highest probability zone. Most S-Rank pulls happen here.
  • Pull 80: ~90% cumulative chance of having pulled an S-Rank.
  • Pull 90: 100% guarantee. Almost nobody reaches this — it's extraordinarily rare.

Practical planning: To guarantee a limited S-Rank character, budget for 160-180 pulls (accounting for losing the 50/50 at soft pity twice). To guarantee at 50/50 win, budget for 80-90 pulls. The realistic "I will probably get them" number is ~150 pulls.

Pity Carry-Over

All pity counters carry over between banners of the same type. If you do 50 pulls on one Exclusive banner and don't get an S-Rank, your next 40 pulls on the next Exclusive banner will hit hard pity. The 50/50 guarantee also carries over — if you lose the 50/50 on this banner, your next S-Rank on any future Exclusive banner is guaranteed featured.

Optimal Pulling Strategy

For F2P Players

  1. Never pull on the Standard Banner with Polychromes. Only use free Master Tapes.
  2. Never build pity. Every pull on a banner you don't want is gambling. Only pull when you want the featured 5-star.
  3. Prioritize agents over W-Engines. A new agent enables new team compositions. A W-Engine makes one agent stronger. Agents first, always.
  4. Save 160-180 pulls before pulling for an agent you really want. If you can't guarantee them, be emotionally prepared to lose the 50/50 and walk away.
  5. Skip banners strategically. You cannot pull every agent. Evaluate whether a new agent fills a gap in your roster or is just a sidegrade to what you already have.

For Low Spenders (Inter-Knot Membership + Battle Pass)

  1. Buy the Inter-Knot Membership. It's by far the most efficient Polychrome-to-dollar ratio in the game (3,000 Polychromes for $5 vs standard rates).
  2. Consider W-Engine banners for your main DPS. With extra income, pulling signature W-Engines for your most-used DPS agents becomes reasonable.
  3. Use Residual Signals wisely. Exchange for Encrypted Master Tapes rather than agents unless you need a specific A-Rank to complete a team.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How does the pity system work in Zenless Zone Zero?

ZZZ has a pity system that guarantees high-rarity items after a certain number of pulls. Hard pity for S-Rank agents on the Exclusive Channel (character banner) is 90 pulls, with soft pity starting around pull 75 where rates increase dramatically. Hard pity for S-Rank W-Engines is 80 pulls with soft pity at 65. Pity counters carry over between banners of the same type. The 50/50 system means your first S-Rank on the Exclusive banner has a 50% chance of being the featured rate-up agent — if you lose, your next S-Rank is guaranteed featured.

Q: What is the 50/50 system in ZZZ?

When you pull an S-Rank on the Exclusive Channel (character banner), there is a 50% chance it will be the featured rate-up agent and a 50% chance it will be a random Standard S-Rank agent (like Lycaon, Koleda, Nekomata, Grace, Rina, or Soldier 11). If you lose the 50/50 (get a Standard agent), your next S-Rank on any Exclusive banner is 100% guaranteed to be the featured rate-up agent. This guarantee carries over between banners indefinitely.

Q: How many Polychromes do F2P players earn per patch?

F2P players earn approximately 12,000-15,000 Polychromes per patch cycle (6 weeks), which translates to roughly 75-94 pulls. This income comes from daily activities, events, Shiyu Defense, Hollow Zero, story content, and maintenance compensation. With the Inter-Knot Membership ($5/month), income increases by about 3,000 Polychromes per patch.

Q: Should I pull on the Beginner Banner?

Yes, absolutely. The Beginner Banner costs only 8 Master Tapes per 10-pull instead of 10 (a 20% discount), and you are guaranteed one S-Rank agent within 50 pulls. After 50 pulls, the banner disappears. Use free Master Tapes earned from early game progression to complete this banner — it's the best value in the early game.

Q: Does pity carry over between character and W-Engine banners?

No. Character banner pity and W-Engine banner pity are separate tracks. Pulls on the Exclusive Channel do not count toward W-Engine Channel pity, and vice versa. However, pity does carry over between banners of the same type — for example, pulls from one Exclusive Channel banner carry over to the next Exclusive Channel banner.
