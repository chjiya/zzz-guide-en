Battery Charge (Stamina) Guide 🔋

📌 v1.0 • Updated June 4, 2026 • Resource Management • Daily • Farming

Overview

Battery Charge is Zenless Zone Zero's stamina system — the time-gated resource that governs nearly all material farming, agent progression, and equipment upgrades. Every run of the HIA Club's Routine Cleanup (Drive Discs), Combat Simulation (Agent EXP), Notorious Hunt (weekly bosses), and material domains costs Battery Charge, making it the single most important resource for daily account progression.

Effective stamina management is what separates optimized accounts from those that plateau mid-game. Knowing what to farm, when to farm it, and in what order can save weeks of real time and thousands of wasted Battery Charge. This guide breaks down the complete stamina economy — from regeneration rates and caps to daily spending priorities at every stage of the game.

Battery Charge Basics

StatValue
Maximum Capacity240 Battery Charge
Regeneration Rate1 charge per 6 minutes
Regeneration per Hour10 Battery Charge
Regeneration per Day240 Battery Charge
Time to Full (0→240)24 hours exactly
OverflowNone — any regeneration past 240 is lost. Spend before you cap!

Key takeaway: You generate exactly 240 Battery Charge per day. Logging in once every 24 hours is sufficient to avoid waste. However, logging in twice a day (morning and evening) allows you to spend ~120 charge per session and never hit the cap.

Extra Battery Charge Sources

  • Coffee (Daily): Drinking coffee at the Coff Cafe grants +60 Battery Charge once per day. This is functionally a daily stamina boost — never skip it.
  • Battery Charge Packs: Available in the Signal Shop (Polychrome exchange). Each pack restores 60 Battery Charge. Only consider these if you're a spender — they're expensive for F2P.
  • Battle Pass: The premium Battle Pass track awards Battery Charge packs at certain levels.
  • Events: Limited-time events sometimes grant Battery Charge as login rewards or mission completions.

Daily Spending Priority by Inter-Knot Level

Early Game (IK 1-30)

Your goal: Level your main team of 3 agents to 40, unlock Hollow Zero, and build a foundation.

  1. Combat Simulation (Agent EXP): Top priority. Get your 3 main agents to level 40 as fast as possible. Level-gating is the biggest progression blocker early on.
  2. W-Engine Upgrade Materials: Level your DPS agent's W-Engine alongside the agent. The base ATK from W-Engine levels is crucial early damage.
  3. Talent Materials: Only level the most important skills (Basic Attack and EX Special for DPS). Don't spread resources across all skills yet.
  4. Notorious Hunt: Start as soon as it unlocks (IK 30). Even Difficulty I drops are valuable for early Talent upgrades.
  5. DO NOT FARM: Drive Discs. Use whatever drops from story and events. Farming before IK 40 is a massive waste of stamina.

Mid Game (IK 30-40)

Your goal: Push your main DPS to level 50, prepare for IK 40 Drive Disc farming, and start Shiyu Defense.

  1. Combat Simulation: Push your main DPS to level 50. Support and Stun agents can sit at 40-45.
  2. Notorious Hunt (weekly): Spend all 3 free attempts. Upgrade to Difficulty II or III as soon as you can clear them.
  3. Talent Materials: Level your DPS agent's core skills to at least level 6. Prioritize Basic Attack, EX Special, and Chain Attack.
  4. W-Engine Materials: Keep your DPS W-Engine at max level for your IK tier.
  5. Light Drive Disc farming: Once your agents are level 45+, start doing a few Routine Cleanup runs to get A-Rank discs with correct main stats. Don't go overboard — S-Rank farming at IK 40 is far more efficient.

Late Game (IK 40-50)

Your goal: S-Rank Drive Discs on your main team, level 50-60 agents, and Shiyu Defense clears.

  1. Routine Cleanup (Drive Discs): This becomes your primary stamina sink. Farm domains that benefit multiple agents. Get correct main stats on S-Rank discs for your main DPS first, then Stun agent, then Support.
  2. Notorious Hunt: Difficulty III or IV every week. Essential for pushing Talents past level 8.
  3. Combat Simulation: Push your main DPS to level 55-60. The level difference multiplier in Shiyu Defense is significant.
  4. Talent Materials: Push DPS core skills to level 8-10. Stun and Support skills to level 6-8.

Endgame (IK 50+)

Your goal: Optimize substats, build a second Shiyu Defense team, and prepare for new agents.

  1. Routine Cleanup (Drive Discs): 70-80% of your daily stamina. Chase double-crit substats for your main DPS. Build sets for your second team.
  2. Notorious Hunt: Difficulty IV for maximum material drops. Stockpile materials even if you don't need them immediately — new agents will need them.
  3. Pre-farming: When leaks or announcements hint at upcoming agents, pre-farm their Talent materials, W-Engine mats, and EXP books.
  4. Combat Simulation: Only when leveling new agents. Your core roster should already be 60.

What NOT to Spend Stamina On

  • Dennies farming: You get enough Dennies from dailies, events, and Hollow Zero. If you're running out, you're over-leveling too many things at once.
  • Drive Discs before IK 40: The difference between A-Rank and S-Rank discs is enormous. Every Battery Charge spent on discs before IK 40 is effectively half as valuable. Wait.
  • Over-leveling Support and Stun agents: Your Support and Stun agents gain far less from levels 55→60 than your DPS does. Push DPS to 60 first, then circle back.
  • Expert Challenge for agents you don't use: Talent materials are expensive. Only farm for agents currently in your active teams.

Daily Stamina Routine (Optimal)

Here's what a typical endgame day looks like for stamina spending (300 total charge: 240 natural + 60 coffee):

  1. Morning login (5 min): Drink coffee (+60). Spend 3× Routine Cleanup (180 charge). Your regeneration continues while you're away.
  2. Evening login (5 min): Spend the remaining 120 charge: 2× Routine Cleanup or 1× Notorious Hunt (if Monday-Thursday).
  3. If it's Monday: Do all 3 Notorious Hunt runs first (180 charge) before any Routine Cleanup.

This routine takes less than 10 minutes of active play per session and ensures zero stamina waste.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How does Battery Charge (Stamina) work in Zenless Zone Zero?

Battery Charge is Zenless Zone Zero's stamina system. You have a maximum capacity of 240, and it regenerates at a rate of 1 charge every 6 minutes (10 per hour, 240 per 24 hours). It is spent on activities like HIA Club farming, Notorious Hunt, and material domains. The daily coffee gives +60 extra charge.

Q: What should I spend my daily Battery Charge on?

Priority order depends on your account stage. Before IK 40: Agent EXP materials > Talent materials > W-Engine materials. After IK 40: Drive Disc farming (Routine Cleanup) becomes your top priority alongside Notorious Hunt (3× weekly). Always level your main DPS agents, their W-Engines, and their core Talents before farming substats.

Q: How fast does Battery Charge regenerate?

Battery Charge regenerates at 1 charge per 6 minutes, which equals exactly 10 per hour and 240 per full 24-hour period. The cap is hard at 240 — any regeneration past this point is permanently lost. The daily coffee adds 60 extra charge, giving you effectively 300 total stamina per day.

Q: Should I use Polychromes to refresh stamina?

Not recommended for F2P or low-spenders. Polychromes are far more valuable for pulling agents and W-Engines. The 300 daily stamina (with coffee) is sufficient for steady progression. Only light spenders who buy the Inter-Knot Membership and Battle Pass should consider occasional stamina refreshes, and even then, only the first refresh (50 Polychromes) is remotely efficient.

Q: When should I start farming Drive Discs?

Start seriously farming Drive Discs at Inter-Knot Level 40, when S-Rank (gold) discs become a guaranteed drop per Routine Cleanup run. Before IK 40, use whatever A-Rank and B-Rank discs drop from story, events, and Hollow Zero. The efficiency gap between pre-IK 40 and post-IK 40 disc farming is so large that earlier farming is effectively throwing stamina away.
