W-Engine System Guide ⚙️

📌 v1.0 • Updated June 4, 2026 • Weapons • Gacha • Crafting

Overview

W-Engines are Zenless Zone Zero's weapon system — signature pieces of technology that agents equip to dramatically enhance their combat capabilities. Think of them as the "weapon" in a traditional gacha RPG: each W-Engine provides a primary stat (ATK%, CRIT Rate, Impact, Energy Regen, or Anomaly Proficiency) and a unique passive effect that synergizes with specific agent playstyles, roles, and factions.

A W-Engine can make or break an agent's performance. An agent with their BiS (Best-in-Slot) Signature W-Engine will deal approximately 15-25% more damage than the same agent using the best F2P alternative. However, good A-Rank options — particularly from the Battle Pass, Signal Shop, or crafting — are more than sufficient to clear all current content, including Shiyu Defense Floor 10.

Understanding W-Engine mechanics — rarity tiers, overclocking (refinement), crafting, and how to evaluate passive effects — is critical for efficient account progression and knowing when it's worth pulling on the W-Engine banner versus saving for new agents.

Key Stats

Each W-Engine provides three stat components:

  • Base ATK: Scales with W-Engine level. Higher rarity = higher base ATK at equivalent levels.
  • Secondary Stat: A percentage stat that scales with level: ATK%, CRIT Rate, CRIT DMG, Impact, Energy Regen, or Anomaly Proficiency. This is often the main reason to choose one engine over another.
  • Passive Effect: A unique ability with a trigger condition (e.g., "after using an EX Special Attack", "when HP is above 80%"). The effect strength increases with Overclocking (duplicate copies).

Overclocking (Refinement)

Overclocking is the process of enhancing a W-Engine's passive effect using duplicate copies. Each Overclock level (★1 through ★5) increases the strength of the passive effect. Here's what you need to know:

  • Level vs. Overclock: Leveling increases base ATK and secondary stat. Overclocking ONLY improves the passive effect. Both are independent systems.
  • S-Rank Overclocking: Each duplicate S-Rank engine costs significant pulls. ★1 (one dupe) is a modest 10-15% improvement. ★5 is for whales only — the cost is enormous.
  • A-Rank Overclocking: Much more accessible since A-Rank engines are common from all banners. Aiming for ★3-5 on your favorite A-Rank engine is realistic even for F2P players over time.
  • Overclock Priority: Overclock your main DPS's engine first. The damage gain is most impactful there. Stun and Support engines gain proportionally less from Overclocking.

W-Engine Banner vs Agent Banner

One of the most common questions: should you pull on the W-Engine banner or save for new agents?

  • Pull W-Engine if: (1) You main the agent and want their absolute best performance. (2) You already have the agent and have enough pulls to guarantee the engine (hard pity at 80). (3) The 75/25 rate on W-Engine banners is more favorable than the 50/50 on character banners.
  • Skip W-Engine if: (1) You're a new player who needs more agents for team variety. (2) A strong A-Rank or Battle Pass alternative exists (e.g., Cannon Rotor vs Hailstorm Shrine). (3) You're saving for upcoming agents you want more.
  • Gold standard rule: Agents > W-Engines. A new agent expands your team-building options. A W-Engine makes one agent stronger. Unless you're committed to maining an agent long-term, the agent is the better pull.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is a W-Engine in Zenless Zone Zero?

A W-Engine is Zenless Zone Zero's weapon system — a signature piece of equipment that defines your agent's fighting capabilities. Each W-Engine provides a powerful main stat (ATK%, CRIT Rate, Impact, Energy Regen, or Anomaly Proficiency) and a unique passive effect that synergizes with specific agent playstyles and roles.

Q: Should I pull for an agent's signature W-Engine?

Signature W-Engines are typically 15-25% stronger than the best F2P alternatives. They are worth pulling if you main the agent and have already secured the agent themselves. However, for most players, good A-Rank alternatives (Battle Pass engines, craftable engines) provide enough power to clear all content including Shiyu Defense Floor 10.

Q: How do I overclock (refine) a W-Engine?

Overclocking increases a W-Engine's passive effect strength (up to 5 stars). Each overclock level requires a duplicate copy of the same W-Engine or a Universal Overclock Chip (rare event item). The base stats from level remain unchanged — only the passive effect improves. Overclock your main DPS's engine first for maximum impact.

Q: What's the best F2P W-Engine for Attack agents?

The craftable Starlight Engine is the best pure F2P option for Attack agents, providing ATK% main stat and an ATK buff after performing a Dodge Counter or Assist. If you purchase the Battle Pass, Cannon Rotor (BP level 30) is significantly better with its CRIT Rate main stat and periodic extra damage proc.

Q: Can I craft S-Rank W-Engines?

No. S-Rank W-Engines can only be obtained through the gacha system (Limited W-Engine banner or Standard banner). However, the Box Galaxy crafting system allows you to create excellent A-Rank engines like Starlight Engine (Attack), Steam Oven (Stun), and Slice of Time (Support) using farmable materials from weekly bosses and Hollow Zero.
