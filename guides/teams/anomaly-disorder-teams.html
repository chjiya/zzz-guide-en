Understanding Disorder: How It Works

Disorder is Zenless Zone Zero's most powerful elemental reaction mechanic for Anomaly-focused teams. When two different Anomaly effects are active on the same enemy simultaneously, one of them triggers a Disorder proc — dealing a burst of damage equal to 75% of the accumulated Anomaly damage that would have ticked over the remaining duration, plus a flat base multiplier that scales with character level and Anomaly Proficiency.

The core loop of Disorder teams is simple in theory but requires precise execution:

  • Step 1: Apply Anomaly A (e.g., Burn — Fire anomaly) to the enemy by building up the element's Anomaly meter to 100%.
  • Step 2: Switch to your second Anomaly character and build up Anomaly B (e.g., Assault — Physical anomaly) to 100% while Burn is still ticking.
  • Step 3: When Anomaly B procs on an enemy that already has Anomaly A active, Disorder triggers. The remaining tick damage of the first anomaly is paid out as immediate burst damage, plus a bonus multiplier.
  • Step 4: Anomaly A is consumed. Repeat by reapplying it on top of Anomaly B to chain Disorders.

💡 Key Insight: The Disorder damage formula is roughly: Disorder Damage = (Remaining ticks × tick damage × 0.75) + (ATK × Anomaly Proficiency × 0.012 × LevelMultiplier). This means higher Anomaly Proficiency (AP) and ATK directly scale your Disorder procs. With 400+ AP, a single Disorder can deal 80,000–150,000 damage depending on enemy RES and DEF.

The most important stat for Disorder teams is Anomaly Proficiency (AP), which increases the damage of both the initial Anomaly application and the subsequent Disorder proc. Anomaly Mastery (AM) is secondary but critical — it accelerates how fast you fill the Anomaly meter, directly controlling your rotation speed. A good rule of thumb: aim for 350+ AP before investing heavily in ATK% on your main Anomaly DPS.

📊 Disorder Damage Comparison at Level 60 (400 AP, 2500 ATK, 10% RES): Burn (Fire) → Assault (Phys): ~95,000 damage per proc. Shock (Electric) → Burn (Fire): ~88,000 damage per proc. Frost (Ice) → Shock (Electric): ~102,000 damage per proc (highest due to Ice RES shred).

Miyabi / Promeia — Frost Disorder (Ice Variant)

The Frost element, introduced with Miyabi and later expanded with Promeia, brings a unique variant to Disorder teams. Frost Anomaly applies a stacking slow and increases Ice DMG taken by the target. When Frost procs Disorder with another element, the Disorder damage receives a bonus multiplier tied to the number of Frost stacks accumulated — making Frost Disorder the highest-damage-per-proc of any elemental combination.

Miyabi remains the premier Frost DPS as of v2.8. Her playstyle focuses on stacking Frost marks through her enhanced basic attacks and EX Special, then detonating them for massive burst damage. Promeia serves as a secondary Frost sub-DPS who can maintain Frost application while Miyabi is off-field building stacks.

Drive Disc Recommendations for Disorder Teams

Drive Disc selection is critical for maximizing Disorder team damage. Unlike crit-based teams, Anomaly characters prioritize Anomaly Proficiency and Anomaly Mastery over traditional offensive stats. Here is the stat priority breakdown per role:

Anomaly DPS (Main): AP% (Slot 4) > Elemental DMG% (Slot 5) > ATK% (Slot 6). Substats: AP > ATK% > Energy Regen > Flat ATK. Aim for 350-400 AP minimum before investing in ATK%. With 450+ AP, consider swapping Slot 5 to ATK% if your team provides external Elemental DMG buffs (like Caesar's ATK buff indirectly scaling Disorder damage).

Anomaly Sub-DPS (Off-field): AP% (Slot 4) > ATK% (Slot 5) > Energy Regen (Slot 6). Substats: Energy Regen ≥ AP > ATK%. Off-field characters like Burnice and Promeia need enough Energy Regen to maintain their off-field application uptime — aim for 2.0+ Energy Regen before heavy AP investment.

Shield/Support (Caesar): Impact (Slot 6) is the priority for faster Daze. Slot 4 HP% or DEF%, Slot 5 DEF% or HP%. Substats: Impact > Energy Regen > DEF% > HP%. Caesar's shield scales with DEF, so prioritize DEF% main stats where possible.

💡 Pro Tip: The 4-piece Chaos Jazz set on Burnice (and Promeia) provides 15% off-field DMG bonus, which directly increases Afterburn damage. For on-field Anomaly DPS like Jane Doe and Yanagi, 4-piece Thunder Metal (Yanagi) or 4-piece Fanged Metal (Jane) provides the best overall damage multiplier. Always prioritize the 4-piece bonus — the 2-piece secondary is flexible.

Advanced Disorder Tips for v2.8

  • Rotation order matters more than you think. Always apply the slower-building Anomaly first. For Burnice + Jane: apply Burn first (faster buildup) then build Assault. This ensures Assault's higher base damage triggers the Disorder.
  • Watch enemy Anomaly RES. Some enemies have specific Anomaly RES values. The Pompeii mech boss has 50% Electric RES but 0% Physical RES — bring Jane against it instead of Yanagi.
  • Maximize your Disorder chain. In optimal play, you want to alternate Anomaly applications: Burn → Assault → Disorder → Reapply Burn → Reapply Assault → Disorder again. Each cycle should take 10-14 seconds with good Anomaly Mastery.
  • Don't let both Anomalies expire. If you let both Anomaly effects run out before applying a new one, you lose the Disorder window entirely. Maintain at least one Anomaly on the target at all times.
  • Caesar's shield is a damage multiplier. Beyond survivability, Caesar's shield activates the 15% ATK buff from many W-Engines and drives. Never underestimate how much uptime contributes to sustained Disorder DPS.
