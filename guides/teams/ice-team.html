🧊 Ice Element Overview

The Ice (Frost/Frozen) element in Zenless Zone Zero is one of the strongest and most versatile damage types in the game. It centers around two distinct mechanics that work together: Freeze (crowd control via Anomaly accumulation) and Frost (Promeia's unique sub-element that triggers an Ice-element Disorder).

Frozen Anomaly is the standard Ice anomaly effect. When you accumulate enough Ice Anomaly on an enemy (by hitting them with Ice attacks), they become Frozen — this locks them in place for a few seconds and deals bonus damage scaling with the agent's Anomaly Mastery and ATK. Frozen enemies take increased Ice damage from all sources during the frozen window. This makes Ice teams exceptionally strong against aggressive enemies and bosses that can be crowd-controlled.

Frost is Promeia's unique mechanic added in version 2.8. It functions as a second Ice-aligned anomaly that builds up independently of the standard Frozen bar. When Frost triggers, it deals massive burst damage and can also trigger Disorder reactions with other anomalies including standard Frozen. Promeia's Frost accumulation rate is among the highest in the game, making her the premier Ice anomaly engine.

Core Strengths of Ice:

  • Crowd Control: Freeze locks dangerous enemies, stopping charge attacks and giving you safe DPS windows
  • Frost Disorder: Promeia's Frost mechanic enables devastating Disorder procs that rival full anomaly teams
  • Strong F2P Core: Soukaku (A-rank, free) is one of the best supports in the game, and Lycaon provides universal Ice RES shred
  • Boss Performance: Ice teams perform well against most endgame bosses due to Freeze's interrupt potential
  • Elemental Flexibility: Ice works well as a mono-element or as a Disorder hybrid with Electric (shock-freeze combos) or Physical

Weaknesses: Ice teams can struggle against enemies immune to Freeze (certain bosses have freeze immunity). Some newer enemy types in v2.8+ have high Ice RES or Freeze resistance. In those cases, consider switching to a Disorder hybrid comp instead of mono-Ice.

❄️ Miyabi Frost Disorder Team (Top Meta)

Team: Miyabi (Main DPS) + Promeia (Frost Engine) + Soukaku (Support)

This is the premium Ice team and one of the strongest compositions in the entire game as of v1.4. The core loop works through a dual-anomaly engine: Promeia accumulates the Frost bar rapidly while Miyabi builds her Frostburn stacks. When Promeia triggers a Frost explosion, it counts as an Ice-element Disorder, which immediately triggers Miyabi's Frostburn passive for massive bonus damage.

Why This Team Works:

  • Promeia's Frost accumulation is the fastest anomaly build-up in the Ice roster — she can trigger Frost once every 8-10 seconds with proper rotations
  • Miyabi's Frostburn DoT ticks for 15-30k per tick depending on investment, and each Disorder proc resets the duration
  • Soukaku provides a massive 30-50% ATK buff (scaling with her own ATK) that benefits both Miyabi and Promeia
  • The team has virtually no downtime — someone always has a skill ready

Rotation: Soukaku buff → Promeia EX Special to build Frost → swap to Miyabi for Frostburn application → drop back to Promeia for another Frost cycle → repeat. Use Soukaku's buff every 15 seconds and reapply Miyabi's debuff as needed.

Required Investment: Both S-rank agents at minimum M0 with signature W-Engines recommended. Soukaku at C6 (M6) is ideal but not required. This team can clear Shiyu Defense Critical Node floors in 90-120 seconds with moderate investment.

🦈 Ellen Joe Hypercarry Team

Team: Ellen Joe (Main DPS) + Lycaon (Stun/RES Shred) + Soukaku (Support/Buffer)

The classic Ice hypercarry team that has been meta since launch. Ellen Joe delivers massive burst damage through her Afterimage mechanic, while Lycaon provides the stun window and Ice RES shred that doubles her damage during the stun phase.

Key Mechanics:

  • Ellen builds Afterimage stacks by landing her third basic attack string and using her dash attack. Each stack increases the damage of her next EX Special
  • Lycaon's EX Special applies 25% Ice RES shred for 12 seconds — this should be reapplied before every stun window
  • Stun windows are the primary damage phase: stun the enemy with Lycaon → apply Soukaku's ATK buff → dump all of Ellen's EX Specials and Ultimate
  • Ellen's Ultimate should be saved for stun windows where the Soukaku buff and Lycaon shred are both active

Rotation: Lycaon EX Special (RES shred) → Soukaku Fly the Flag + EX Special (ATK buff) → Lycaon basic attacks and counters for stun → during stun: refresh Soukaku buff → Ellen EX Special spam + Ultimate. Reset and repeat.

F2P Viability: This team works well with Ellen at M0 (or even a borrowed Ellen) with the Starlight Engine W-Engine (craftable). Lycaon can be replaced with any S-rank stun agent (Qingyi works well as a neutral stunner). Soukaku is free. Total S-rank requirement: 1-2.

🌫️ Promeia Frost Freeze Team

Team: Promeia (Frost DPS) + Lycaon (Stun/RES Shred) + Soukaku (Support)

Promeia's Frost mechanic enables a unique playstyle that mixes sustained Frost damage with freeze crowd control. While she works best as a Disorder partner for Miyabi, she is also a perfectly capable main DPS in her own right.

How Frost Works: Promeia has a hidden Frost meter (similar to an Anomaly bar) that fills when she attacks with her EX Special, chain attacks, and Ultimate. When the Frost bar fills, it explodes for area Ice damage and counts as an Elemental Disorder for all existing anomalies on the target. If the enemy is already Frozen (standard Ice anomaly), the Frost explosion triggers a double Disorder — both the Frost explosion and a bonus Disorder damage proc.

Team Strategy: Lycaon builds stun and applies Ice RES shred while his basic attacks contribute to Freeze accumulation. When the enemy is about to be stunned, use Soukaku to buff → Promeia EX Special to build Frost → stun → Promeia Ultimate (which guarantees a Frost explosion) → follow up with chain attacks. The Frost explosion during stun window creates massive burst damage.

Budget Alternative: Replace Lycaon with any stun agent (Anby works well). The RES shred loss is significant (roughly 15-20% team DPS) but the core Frost loop still functions. This is a solid 1 S-rank team.

🆓 F2P & Budget Ice Teams

Building an Ice team without multiple S-rank agents requires creativity, but it's definitely possible. Here are budget alternatives:

Budget Option 1: Soukaku + Anby + Support DPS

Use Soukaku as the main Ice enabler with Anby (A-rank Electric Stun, free) for stuns and a borrowed or single S-rank Ice DPS. Anby provides reliable stuns with good energy generation, while Soukaku handles Ice application and buffing. This team works well in Shiyu Defense floors 1-5 and all story content.

Budget Option 2: Soukaku + Nicole + Any Ice DPS

Nicole (A-rank Ether Support) provides DEF shred and energy generation that benefits any team. With Soukaku's ATK buff and Nicole's DEF shred, even a poorly-geared Ice DPS can deal solid damage. This is the cheapest viable Ice team — all A-rank agents except your DPS.

Budget Option 3: Dual A-Rank + Stunner

Soukaku + Nicole + Anby is a triple A-rank team that can clear early-to-mid game content using disorder-like reactions (Freeze + Ether application). It won't clear endgame Shiyu Defense, but it's more than adequate for story progression up to Inter-Knot level 45.

General F2P Advice: Prioritize building Soukaku to C6 (M6) — her C6 gives an extra 10% Ice DMG bonus on top of her ATK buff, which dramatically improves all Ice team compositions. Soukaku is an A-rank agent, so you will eventually C6 her through standard banner pulls and shop rotations.

🔄 Rotation Guide

Here are the optimal rotation sequences for the two premier Ice teams. Practice these in Hollow Zero to build muscle memory.

Miyabi + Promeia Rotation

  1. Opener: Start with Soukaku. Hold her EX Special (Fly the Flag) to enter the buff state. Tap EX Special again after the flag animation to apply the ATK buff to the whole team.
  2. Frost Setup: Swap to Promeia. Use her EX Special 2-3 times to build the Frost bar to 70-80%. Don't trigger Frost yet — you want to time it.
  3. Miyabi Entry: Swap to Miyabi. Use her dash attack + basic combo to build Frostburn stacks on the enemy. Apply Frostburn debuff (3 stacks = maximum DoT damage).
  4. Frost Trigger: Swap back to Promeia. One more EX Special triggers Frost explosion → this counts as a Disorder → Miyabi's Frostburn passive activates for bonus damage.
  5. Loop: Repeat steps 1-4. Use Ultimates immediately when available (prioritize Promeia Ultimate for guaranteed Frost trigger).

Ellen + Lycaon Rotation

  1. Opener: Lycaon EX Special → Soukaku Fly the Flag → Lycaon stun building (basic attacks + perfect assists)
  2. Pre-Stun Prep: When stun is at 80-90%, swap to Soukaku and re-apply the ATK buff. Then swap to Lycaon for the final stun hit.
  3. Stun Window: Swap to Ellen. Use 1-2 basic combos to build 2-3 Afterimage stacks. Then spam EX Special+ (enhanced by Afterimages). If Ultimate is ready, use it at the start of the stun window for maximum benefit.
  4. Cooldown: After stun ends, use Lycaon to rebuild energy and reapply RES shred. Soukaku maintains the ATK buff. Ellen builds energy through basic attacks.

Pro Tip: In both rotations, use chain attacks (triggered by stun or anomaly) to extend your damage window. Chain attacks also provide i-frames that can dodge enemy attacks during important setups. Always use the agent with the highest damage contribution last in the chain attack order to maximize field time for your main DPS.

❓ FAQ

Q1: Which Ice team is better — Miyabi or Ellen?

At equal investment (M0W1 each), Miyabi + Promeia deals roughly 20-30% more total damage than Ellen + Lycaon in sustained fights. However, Ellen's team is significantly easier to play and more forgiving of rotation mistakes. For Shiyu Defense speed clears, Miyabi wins. For general farming and story content, Ellen is more comfortable. Both are strong enough to clear all content — pick based on which playstyle you prefer.

Q2: Is Promeia worth pulling just for Ice teams?

Yes, absolutely. Promeia is the single biggest upgrade to any Ice team in v2.8+. Even at M0, her Frost mechanic doubles the Ice team's Disorder damage potential. She works as both a main DPS in her own team and as a sub-DPS/anomaly engine for Miyabi. Her value is comparable to how much Caesar improves physical teams. Recommended priority: Promeia > her signature W-Engine if you're on a budget.

Q3: Can I run Ice without Lycaon?

Yes. Lycaon's Ice RES shred is a 25% damage amplifier for Ice damage — roughly equivalent to an extra Drive Disc set bonus. Replacing him with Qingyi (Electric Stun) or Anby (A-rank Electric Stun) means losing that multiplier, but the core Ice damage loop still functions. The team will be approximately 15-20% weaker against Ice-neutral enemies. Against enemies already weak to Ice, the loss is less noticeable since base damage is already high.

Q4: What W-Engines should I use for Ice teams?

Miyabi: Signature W-Engine (Frostblade) > Ice-Jade Teapot (Qingyi's sig) is decent stat stick > Starlight Engine (craftable). Promeia: Signature (recommended for Frost rate) > Weeping Cradle (standard) > Rainforest Gourmet (craftable). Ellen: Signature (Deep Sea Visitor) > Marcato Desire (battle pass) > Star Collider (craftable). Lycaon: Ice-Jade Teapot > The Restrained (standard) > Precious Fossilized Core (craftable). Soukaku: Bashful Demon (craftable, best for team ATK buff) > any support W-Engine with ATK% secondary.

Q5: Are there any Ice-Disorder hybrid teams that mix elements?

Yes! Ice + Electric (Shock + Freeze) creates a strong quickswap Disorder team. Use Promeia + Yanagi (Electric Anomaly) + Soukaku. Promeia builds Frost while Yanagi builds Shock — when Promeia triggers Frost, it Disorders the Shock for massive damage, and vice versa. This dual-anomaly team can rival Miyabi teams in single-target damage but requires more precise rotation management. Similarly, Ice + Fire (Promeia + Burnice) works well if you need Fire coverage.
