Ice Team Compositions Guide ❄️

📌 v1.0 • Updated June 7, 2026 • Ice • Endgame • Freeze & Shatter

Understanding Ice Teams in ZZZ

Ice teams in Zenless Zone Zero are defined by one of the most oppressive crowd-control mechanics in the game: Freeze. Ice's Anomaly effect completely immobilizes enemies for a duration, and when the Freeze ends — whether by natural expiration or being broken by a heavy attack — it triggers Shatter, a powerful burst of additional damage. This dual-phase mechanic (crowd control into bonus damage) gives Ice teams exceptional safety and damage output simultaneously, making them the strongest element in the current meta.

The Ice roster is headlined by the undisputed T0 queen of ZZZ: Hoshimi Miyabi. Her unique Frostburn mechanic is what separates her from every other agent in the game — Frostburn ignores 30% of the target's Ice RES and deals massive Anomaly damage with high base multipliers. Unlike traditional Attack agents who rely on Stun windows for their damage spikes, Miyabi's damage profile is Anomaly-driven, meaning she deals devastating damage continuously throughout the fight regardless of whether the enemy is Stunned. She applies Freeze rapidly, triggers Shatter with every rotation, and her damage ceiling exceeds every other agent in v1.0.

But Ice isn't just Miyabi. Ellen Joe is a premium S-Rank Ice Attack agent with a unique charged-scissor mechanic that delivers tremendous burst damage during Stun windows. Von Lycaon is the premium Ice Stun agent whose Additional Ability applies a damage multiplier to Stunned enemies — a direct amplification of every Ice DPS's burst potential. And Soukaku is the A-Rank Ice Support who single-handedly enables Ice teams with her team-wide ATK transfer and Ice DMG Bonus buffs. Together, these four agents form the most synergistic elemental roster in v1.0.

The core mechanic Ice teams revolve around is the Freeze → Shatter loop. Applying enough Ice Anomaly Buildup triggers Freeze, immobilizing the target. When Freeze is broken by a powerful attack (such as a Chain Attack during a Stun window or an Ultimate), Shatter deals a burst of bonus damage that scales with the triggering attack's multipliers. Smart players deliberately time their biggest hits to coincide with the moment Freeze breaks, effectively doubling the damage of their strongest abilities. Mastering this timing is the key difference between a good Ice player and a great one.

The Polar Metal Drive Disc set is the foundational gear piece for every Ice DPS. Its 4-piece bonus grants 20% Ice DMG baseline, plus an additional 20% Ice DMG against Frozen or Shattered enemies — for a total of 40% Ice DMG when conditions are met. Combined with Soukaku's Ice DMG Bonus buff from her Additional Ability, a well-geared Ice DPS can exceed 100% Ice DMG Bonus during active windows. This is why Ice teams scale so dramatically with investment.

Why it's S-Tier: This is the classic premium Ice team for players who have Ellen Joe instead of (or alongside) Miyabi. Ellen's damage profile rewards precise timing — you stack Flash Freeze Trance charges through her sprint and EX Special, then unleash charged scissor attacks during Stun windows for massive burst damage. Lycaon's damage vulnerability debuff and rapid Stun application create the ideal Stun windows, while Soukaku's buffs ensure Ellen's charged attacks hit as hard as possible. Triple Ice synergy activates all Additional Abilities.

Ellen Stack Management: Ellen's skill ceiling comes from her Flash Freeze Trance mechanic. She gains stacks by sprinting (hold dodge to enter shark-dash) or using her EX Special. Each stack enhances her next charged Basic Attack into a Roaming Hunt (multi-hit scissor slash). During a Stun window, you want 3 stacks ready before triggering Chain Attacks. Use EX Special → Chain Attack → charged scissor x3 → Ultimate for maximum burst damage. Mismanaging stacks — wasting them outside Stun windows or running out mid-burst — is the most common Ellen piloting error.

Weakness: Ellen requires more mechanical precision than Miyabi. Stack management, timing charged attacks within Stun windows, and positioning for her sweeping attacks demand practice. Her sustained damage outside Stun windows is significantly lower than Miyabi's continuous Frostburn output.

Freeze & Shatter Mechanics Deep Dive

Understanding the Freeze → Shatter loop is essential for maximizing Ice team damage:

  • Freeze Duration: Freeze immobilizes enemies for a base duration that scales with the triggering agent's Anomaly Proficiency. Higher Anomaly Proficiency = longer Freeze = more time to set up burst combos.
  • Shatter Trigger: Shatter occurs when Freeze is broken — either by a heavy attack (Chain Attack, Ultimate, charged attack) or when the Freeze duration expires naturally. The damage of Shatter scales with the power of the attack that breaks the Freeze.
  • Deliberate Shattering: The optimal strategy is to time your strongest attack to break Freeze. For Miyabi, this means using her Ultimate at the moment Freeze is about to expire. For Ellen, this means landing her final charged scissor attack as the Freeze breaks. A well-timed Shatter can deal more bonus damage than the attack itself.
  • Multi-Freeze Cycling: With two or more Ice agents (e.g., Miyabi + Soukaku + Lycaon), Freeze can be applied back-to-back. Soukaku applies the first Freeze → Miyabi shatters it with her Ultimate → Lycaon's Chain Attack during the Stun window applies a second Freeze → Miyabi shatters again with EX Special. Each Shatter triggers Polar Metal's conditional bonus and deals burst damage.
  • Frostburn Interaction: Miyabi's Frostburn counts as a separate debuff from Freeze. Frostburn ignores Ice RES and deals damage over time independently. When Freeze and Frostburn are both active, enemies take simultaneous damage from both effects plus the eventual Shatter — this triple-dip is what makes Miyabi teams so dominant.

Investment Priority for Ice Teams

  • Polar Metal Disc Domain: Farm this first. Both Miyabi and Ellen require 4-piece Polar Metal. The domain also drops Freedom Blues (for Soukaku) — making it doubly efficient. Aim for CRIT and ATK% substats. Miyabi also values Anomaly Proficiency substats for faster Frostburn application.
  • Soukaku's Fly the Flag Level: Level Soukaku's Core Skill as high as possible. Each level increases the percentage of her ATK transferred to the team. At max Core Skill, she transfers 20% of her ATK plus a substantial Ice DMG Bonus — this is a team-wide damage multiplier that benefits every Ice DPS.
  • Miyabi's W-Engine: Her signature W-Engine (Hailstorm Shrine) provides massive Anomaly Proficiency and Ice DMG bonuses. It is one of the highest-value signature weapons in the game. If you have Miyabi, her W-Engine should be your top gacha priority after securing her.
  • Lycaon's Impact: Lycaon's Stun speed depends entirely on his Impact stat. Max his Core Skill for the Impact bonus, and ensure Disk 6 has Impact% main stat. A slow Lycaon means fewer Stun windows and lower overall team damage.
  • Ellen's CRIT Ratio: Ellen scales heavily with CRIT. Aim for at least 50% CRIT Rate and 120%+ CRIT DMG. Polar Metal's conditional Ice DMG bonus reduces the pressure on Ice DMG% substats, allowing you to focus purely on CRIT from substat rolls.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Why is Hoshimi Miyabi considered T0 in Zenless Zone Zero?

Miyabi is undisputed T0 because of her Frostburn mechanic. Frostburn is a unique debuff that ignores 30% of the target's Ice RES — effectively a 30% damage amplifier that bypasses enemy defenses. Additionally, Frostburn deals massive Anomaly damage over time with high base multipliers, and Miyabi applies both Freeze and Frostburn simultaneously for continuous uptime. Unlike Attack agents who rely on Stun windows for their damage spikes, Miyabi's Anomaly-driven damage profile means she deals near-peak DPS at all times. In practical terms, Miyabi teams clear content 20-30% faster than any other v1.0 composition with equivalent investment. She is the strongest single agent in the game by a significant margin.

Q: Can I play Ice teams without Hoshimi Miyabi?

Absolutely. Ellen Joe is a fully capable S-Rank Ice Attack agent who can lead Ice teams through all current endgame content. Her charged-scissor mechanic rewards precise timing and stack management, delivering tremendous burst damage during Stun windows. Pair her with Soukaku (ATK + Ice DMG buffs) and Lycaon (Stun + damage vulnerability) for a premium-tier triple-Ice composition. If you lack Lycaon, Anby is a reliable free alternative. Ellen's damage ceiling is lower than Miyabi's — she needs Stun windows for her peak output — but a well-invested Ellen team clears Shiyu Defense Critical Node with time to spare. The key difference is playstyle: Ellen demands precision and timing, while Miyabi offers high damage with lower execution requirements.

Q: How should I build Soukaku for Ice teams?

Soukaku is the single most important support for any Ice team. Build her with 4-piece Swing Jazz (Energy Regen + 15% team DMG buff after Chain Attack/Ultimate) and 2-piece Freedom Blues (Anomaly Proficiency for faster Freeze application). For main stats: Disk 4 = ATK% (increases her Fly the Flag transfer amount), Disk 5 = ATK% or Ice DMG% (ATK% is generally better for buff transfer consistency), Disk 6 = Energy Regen% (ensures EX Special is available every rotation) or ATK% for maximum buff strength. Prioritize ATK% and Energy Regen% substats. Her Fly the Flag buff transfers up to 20% of her own ATK to the team — so stacking ATK on Soukaku directly increases your entire team's damage.

Q: What are the best Drive Discs for Ice teams?

The optimal Drive Disc sets for each Ice agent are: Miyabi — 4pc Polar Metal + 2pc Woodpecker Electro (ATK% and Ice DMG stats, Anomaly Proficiency substats); Ellen Joe — 4pc Polar Metal + 2pc Woodpecker Electro (CRIT Rate/DMG and Ice DMG stats); Soukaku — 4pc Swing Jazz + 2pc Freedom Blues (stack ATK% for Fly the Flag transfer, Energy Regen for uptime); Lycaon — 4pc Shockstar Disco + 2pc Swing Jazz (Impact main stat on Disk 6 is mandatory, Ice DMG% on Disk 5). The Polar Metal domain is the most efficient farm for Ice teams since it drops both Polar Metal (for DPS) and Freedom Blues (for Soukaku).

Q: Is Ellen Joe still viable compared to Miyabi in v1.0?

Yes, Ellen Joe is completely viable and remains one of the strongest Attack agents in v1.0. While Miyabi's Frostburn gives her a fundamentally higher damage ceiling and a more forgiving playstyle, Ellen's Stun-window burst damage is among the highest in the game when executed correctly. A well-played Ellen with optimal stack management, proper Chain Attack sequencing, and well-timed Shatters deals damage that competes with Miyabi during Stun windows — the difference is that Miyabi also deals high damage outside Stun windows. In practical terms: Miyabi clears content faster and with less effort, but Ellen clears all content comfortably. If you enjoy the rewarding skill-ceiling of managing Ellen's Flash Freeze Trance stacks and executing perfect Stun-window combos, she's a deeply satisfying agent to master.

Q: What if I don't have any Ice S-Rank agents at all?

If you lack both Miyabi and Ellen, the Budget Freeze team (Piper + Soukaku + Anby/Corin) is your best option. Piper's Physical Anomaly triggers Disorder with Freeze for respectable damage, and Soukaku's universal ATK buff benefits any team. While this won't match premium Ice teams in clear speed, it provides consistent crowd control through Freeze and solid damage through Disorder reactions. Alternatively, consider pivoting to a different elemental team entirely — Fire (Soldier 11), Electric (Grace/Anton), or Physical (Nekomata/Corin) — until you acquire an Ice S-Rank through banners or the standard 300-selector.
