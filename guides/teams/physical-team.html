Understanding Physical Teams in ZZZ

Physical teams in Zenless Zone Zero occupy a unique position in the meta. Unlike elemental teams that rely on RES shred and elemental synergy, Physical teams leverage the Assault anomaly effect — a damage burst that also briefly stuns enemies — to create hybrid compositions that blend traditional Stun-window gameplay with Anomaly-driven damage. The Physical roster is also the most accessible in the game, with three free agents (Corin, Billy, Piper) covering the DPS, AoE, and Anomaly roles respectively.

The strength of Physical teams lies in their flexibility. Physical damage is neutral — no enemy in the game resists it, and no enemy is weak to it (with rare exceptions in specific Shiyu Defense rotations). This means Physical teams can be deployed into any content without worrying about elemental matchups. The Assault anomaly effect provides crowd control through mini-stuns, interrupting enemy attacks and creating openings for DPS agents. In Disorder compositions, Assault + Shock (Grace + Piper) or Assault + Freeze combinations can deal massive percentage-based damage that scales with enemy HP.

The trade-off is that Physical teams lack the explosive elemental synergy that makes mono-Ice or Ether teams so dominant. Without RES shred mechanics (which are element-specific), Physical damage is purely additive rather than multiplicative. This means Physical teams scale more linearly with investment and tend to have lower ceilings than premium elemental comps. However, for F2P players and accounts without multiple limited S-Rank agents, Physical teams offer the most consistent and accessible path to clearing endgame content.

S-Tier Physical Team Compositions

1. Premium Physical: Nekomata + Piper + Lucy

RoleAgentKey Contribution
DPSNekomataDevastating single-target burst. Bonus 40% damage when attacking enemies from behind, which Assault mini-stuns enable perfectly.
AnomalyPiperApplies Assault every 6-8 seconds. Mini-stuns enable Nekomata's back-attack passive. Physical Anomaly Buildup keeps Fanged Metal's ATK buff active.
SupportLucyTeam-wide ATK buff via Guard Boar. Sons of Calydon faction synergy with Piper activates both their Additional Abilities for bonus Physical DMG.

Why it's S-Tier: Perfect Physical synergy across all three agents. Piper's Assault mini-stuns are the key enabler — they freeze enemies for 1-2 seconds, during which Nekomata can reposition behind them and unload her back-attack damage. Lucy's ATK buff has excellent uptime (15s duration, 30s cooldown), and the Sons of Calydon faction synergy provides additional Physical DMG Bonus to both Piper and Lucy. This team excels against single large bosses where Nekomata can consistently hit from behind.

Weakness: Nekomata is a standard banner S-Rank, making her availability RNG-dependent. The team struggles against highly mobile bosses that prevent consistent back-attack positioning.

2. Physical Anomaly Core: Piper + Corin + Lucy

RoleAgentKey Contribution
Anomaly/DPSPiperPrimary on-field. Applies Assault constantly. Her EX Special spin deals massive AoE Anomaly Buildup while providing super armor.
Burst DPSCorinCapitalizes on Assault mini-stuns for safe EX Special chainsaw channels. Synergizes with Fanged Metal discs that stack ATK on Assault triggers.
SupportLucyTeam ATK buff. Sons of Calydon synergy. Her Guard Boar's attacks also apply minor Physical Anomaly Buildup.

This team flips the traditional DPS-Stun-Support structure on its head. Instead of waiting for Stun windows, Piper is your on-field agent who applies constant Assault pressure. Corin swaps in during Assault mini-stuns to deliver her high-damage EX Special burst, then swaps back out. The team deals continuous damage rather than burst cycling, making it particularly effective in Hollow Zero where fights are longer and consistent damage output is valued over timed burst windows.

The rotation is simple: Nicole groups and debuffs → Anby builds Daze → Stun window → Corin burst. At moderate investment, this team outputs 80-120k damage per burst cycle against stunned enemies.

Nicole's black hole pulls enemies together, Piper's spin keeps them grouped and applies Assault, and Billy's cone-shaped Bullet Storm deals devastating damage to the clustered pack. His Ultimate finishes any survivors. In multi-wave content like Hollow Zero and material domains, this team clears faster than most premium compositions.

Physical Disorder Teams

Physical's Assault anomaly synergizes exceptionally well with other elements to trigger Disorder — a reaction that consumes both active Anomaly effects and deals massive burst damage proportional to their remaining durations.

Physical + Shock Disorder: Piper + Grace + Rina/Lucy

Grace applies Shock → Piper applies Assault → Disorder triggers for massive burst. Rina extends Shock duration for easier Disorder timing; Lucy provides ATK buffs. This is the most accessible Disorder team and can compete with S-Tier compositions in Hollow Zero where Disorder's percentage-based damage thrives against high-HP enemies.

Physical + Freeze Disorder: Piper + Lycaon + Soukaku

A premium hybrid that combines Assault and Freeze. Lycaon provides Ice RES shred and Daze, Soukaku provides ATK buffs, and Piper triggers Disorder by applying Assault to Frozen enemies. More expensive but effective against content that requires elemental coverage.

Rotations & Gameplay

Standard Physical Rotation (Corin + Anby + Nicole):

  1. Nicole EX Special to apply Energy Field (DEF shred + grouping)
  2. Anby builds Daze with Basic Attacks and EX Specials
  3. When Daze near full, Nicole refreshes Energy Field
  4. Stun triggers → Chain Attack into Corin
  5. Corin holds EX Special for full chainsaw channel (~80-120k damage at moderate investment)
  6. Corin Ultimate at end of Stun window for final burst
  7. Repeat from step 1

Physical Anomaly Rotation (Piper + Corin + Lucy):

  1. Piper on-field, EX Special immediately to start building Assault
  2. Continue with Basic Attacks to maintain Anomaly Buildup
  3. When Assault triggers (mini-stun), Quick Assist into Corin
  4. Corin uses EX Special for burst damage during mini-stun window
  5. Swap back to Piper, repeat
  6. Lucy refreshes ATK buff whenever available (Guard Boar at start, then on cooldown)

Physical Team Investment Guide

Physical teams benefit from specific investment priorities:

  • Fanged Metal Drive Discs: The 4-piece set's ATK stacking on Assault triggers makes it the backbone of Physical team damage. Farm this domain first and equip on both your DPS and Anomaly agents.
  • Starlight Engine (Craftable): The ATK buff on Dodge Counter is universally valuable for Physical DPS. Craft and refine this W-Engine as your highest F2P priority.
  • Lucy's Cinema Levels: Lucy is the premier Physical Support. Her M2 extends Guard Boar's ATK buff duration; her M4 adds healing. She appears on all banners, so Cinema levels accumulate naturally.
  • Piper's M4 Breakpoint: The Energy restore on Assault triggers fundamentally changes her rotation, enabling near-continuous EX Special usage. Prioritize Piper copies from the gacha.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is the best Physical team in ZZZ?

The best Physical team is Nekomata + Piper + Lucy. Nekomata's back-attack bonus damage is enabled by Piper's Assault mini-stuns which freeze enemies briefly, allowing Nekomata to reposition behind them. Lucy's team-wide ATK buff and Sons of Calydon faction synergy with Piper creates perfect Physical synergy. This team excels at single-target boss encounters in Shiyu Defense and Hollow Zero.

Q: What is the best F2P Physical team?

The best F2P Physical team is Corin (DPS) + Anby (Stun) + Nicole (Support). All three agents are free from the start and cover the essential DPS-Stun-Support roles perfectly. Nicole's black hole groups enemies for Corin's AoE chainsaw burst, and Anby's reliable Stun application ensures consistent burst windows. This team can clear all story content and early Shiyu Defense floors comfortably.

Q: How does Physical Anomaly (Assault) work in teams?

Assault is Physical's Anomaly effect that deals burst damage and briefly stuns the enemy once the Anomaly gauge fills. In team compositions, Assault provides mini-stun windows for DPS agents like Nekomata who deal bonus damage to disabled enemies. It also triggers Disorder when combined with another element's Anomaly effect, dealing massive percentage-based damage that scales with enemy HP.

Q: Should I use a Stun agent in Physical teams?

Traditional Physical teams (Corin/Billy + Anby + Nicole) should use a Stun agent for the 150% Stun damage multiplier. However, Physical Anomaly teams (Piper-focused) can skip the Stun agent since they deal damage continuously through Assault and Disorder rather than burst windows. The Piper + Corin + Lucy team is an example of a successful Physical comp without a dedicated Stun agent.

Q: Are Physical teams viable for endgame Shiyu Defense?

Physical teams are viable for Shiyu Defense floors 1-10, but fall off in Critical Node (floors 11+) compared to premium elemental teams that exploit enemy weaknesses. The lack of Physical RES shred means Physical teams deal base damage without multiplicative amplification. However, a well-invested Nekomata + Piper + Lucy team can still full-clear Critical Node with sufficient gear and skill investment.

Q: What Drive Discs should I farm for Physical teams?

Farm Fanged Metal as your highest priority — its 4-piece set provides Physical DMG Bonus and stacking ATK on Assault triggers, which is the core damage engine for all Physical teams. Piper, Corin, Billy, and Nekomata all want 4-Fanged Metal. The domain also drops Freedom Blues (for Anomaly builds), making it the most efficient domain for Physical team investment across multiple agents.
